# EU AI Act Compliance Guide

Comprehensive framework for EU AI Act compliance (2025-2027).

## Overview

The EU AI Act is the world's first comprehensive AI regulation. It entered into force on 1 August 2024 with phased enforcement through 2027.

## Key Dates

| Date | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Feb 2025 | Prohibited AI practices banned, AI literacy obligations |
| Aug 2025 | GPAI model compliance (transparency, copyright, documentation) |
| Aug 2026 | Full high-risk AI system compliance, Article 50 transparency |
| Aug 2027 | Extended transition for high-risk AI in regulated products |

## Risk Classification

| Risk Level | Examples | Requirements |
|------------|----------|--------------|
| **Unacceptable** | Social scoring, emotion recognition at work, manipulative AI | Prohibited |
| **High** | Biometrics, critical infrastructure, employment, education AI | Full compliance |
| **Limited** | Chatbots, recommendation systems, deepfakes | Transparency only |
| **Minimal** | Games, spam filters | None |

## High-Risk AI Domains (Annex III)

1. **Biometrics** - Remote identification, categorization
2. **Critical Infrastructure** - Energy, transport, water, digital
3. **Education** - Admissions, assessment, proctoring
4. **Employment** - Recruitment, HR decisions, performance
5. **Essential Services** - Credit scoring, insurance, emergency services
6. **Law Enforcement** - Risk assessment, crime prediction
7. **Migration** - Visa, asylum, border control
8. **Justice** - Legal research, dispute resolution

## Penalties

| Violation | Maximum Fine |
|-----------|--------------|
| Prohibited AI deployment | EUR 35M or 7% global turnover |
| High-risk non-compliance | EUR 15M or 3% global turnover |
| Other violations | EUR 7.5M or 1% global turnover |
| False information | EUR 7.5M or 1% global turnover |
