# Major launch GTM coordination

## Context

Coordinate a flagship launch across product, engineering, design, marketing, sales, and support: from a launch tier decision through pre-launch readiness, day-zero execution, and a 30-day post-launch review.

Tier: **pro**. Complexity: **deep**. Group: **role-product-manager**. Persona: **role-product-manager**.

## Outcome

This playbook is done when:

- Launch tier decision signed off.
- Cross-functional readiness checklist green.
- Day-zero ran with no unresolved P0 incidents.
- 30-day post-launch review delivered with decision.

## Steps

### 1. Launch tier decision

Classify the launch into a tier so the org spends proportional energy.

Tasks:
- Score the launch on impact, novelty, audience breadth, and risk.
- Map the score to a launch tier (T1 flagship vs T2 medium vs T3 minor).
- Write a one-paragraph rationale.

Outputs:
- Launch tier decision doc.

Decision gate: Advance once tier is named and at least one VP signs off.

### 2. Readiness

Bring product, eng, design, marketing, sales, support, and ops to a green readiness check.

Tasks:
- Stand up a launch RACI.
- Run a readiness checklist per function.
- Identify and burn down launch blockers daily.

Outputs:
- RACI doc.
- Readiness checklist with green/red status per function.

Decision gate: Advance when every function reports green or has an owner working a red item with a date.

### 3. Messaging

Lock positioning, differentiators, and the explainer story.

Tasks:
- Run a 30-min positioning workshop.
- Draft the one-line, one-paragraph, and one-page explainers.
- Pressure-test against the top 2 competitors.

Outputs:
- Positioning doc.
- 3-tier message hierarchy.

Decision gate: Advance when messaging passes a sales-team 'can I sell this in 90 seconds' read.

### 4. Channel plan

Pick the launch surfaces and sequence them.

Tasks:
- Choose channels (PH, HN, IH, ASO, owned email, partner relays).
- Stagger the channel hits across day -7 → +14.
- Brief each channel owner on copy and timing.

Outputs:
- Channel plan with owners and dates.

Decision gate: Advance when each channel has a named owner and a draft asset.

### 5. Pre-launch dry run

Rehearse day-zero end-to-end before the spotlight.

Tasks:
- Test the signup → first-value loop with 5 strangers.
- Rehearse the day-zero ops timeline.
- Verify support has triage scripts ready.

Outputs:
- Dry-run report.
- Day-zero ops timeline.

Decision gate: Advance when the dry-run hits no P0 surprises.

### 6. Day zero

Run the launch live with a war room.

Tasks:
- Open a war room with on-call from product + eng + marketing + support.
- Post the launch in scheduled order.
- Triage incoming feedback in real time.

Outputs:
- War-room log.
- Day-zero metrics snapshot.

Decision gate: Advance once the launch window closes with no unresolved P0 incidents.

### 7. 30-day review

Read what happened against the launch-tier expectations.

Tasks:
- Pull metrics vs pre-launch targets.
- Run a structured post-launch retro.
- Decide go-deeper, hold, or sunset.

Outputs:
- 30-day post-launch review doc.
- Sunset/hold/double-down decision.

Decision gate: Required: a written post-launch verdict at day 30.

## Decision points

- T1 flagship vs T2 medium — T1 gets full GTM machinery; T2 gets 30% of it.
- Stagger vs simultaneous launches — stagger if support capacity is thin; simultaneous to dominate the news cycle.
- Hold vs sunset vs double-down — base the call on the 30-day primary metric, not on team feelings.
