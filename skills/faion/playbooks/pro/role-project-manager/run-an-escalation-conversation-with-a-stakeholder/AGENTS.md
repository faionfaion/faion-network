# Run an escalation conversation with a stakeholder

## Context

A live engagement is about to slip on scope, schedule, or quality, and the
project manager has spotted it before steerco. This playbook is the small
working session that turns a red-flag risk into a stakeholder-owned decision
without burning the relationship. It is built for PMs running P4 outsource
engagements or P6 internal squads where the decision authority sits with a
client or executive sponsor outside the PM's direct chain.

## Outcome

Twenty to forty-five minutes after starting, you walk away with a concrete
decision logged in the project record, a named owner, a scheduled follow-up
checkpoint, and updated risk register. The stakeholder can restate the next
step out loud. The decision survives turnover because it lives in writing.

## Steps

1. **Prep with evidence, not feelings.** Pull the risk + change-control
   history for the affected scope. Draft 2-3 mutually exclusive options
   ranked by trade-off. Pre-fill the decision template (who decides, by
   when, what). Walk in with a one-page brief.
2. **Open with the ask in one sentence.** First 60 seconds: name the
   problem, tie impact to a metric the stakeholder cares about (revenue,
   launch date, headcount), no blame language.
3. **Force shared framing.** Ask the stakeholder to restate the problem in
   their own words. If they minimize it, return to evidence; do not move
   on. Move forward only when they own the framing.
4. **Walk options with costs visible.** For each option: cost, time, scope
   impact, residual risk. State your recommendation and why. Invite a
   fourth option from the stakeholder; their idea increases buy-in.
5. **Fill the decision template on screen.** Make the decision concrete
   enough that the next person down the chain could act on it without
   asking you a follow-up question. Name owner, deadline, and a rollback
   condition.
6. **Lock the follow-up before you leave.** Schedule the checkpoint on the
   calendar in the room. Capture action items with owners and due dates.
   Confirm the escalation path if the decision later needs to change.
7. **Log the decision within 24 hours.** Update the risk register entry
   with the new status and a link to the decision. Post a summary in the
   project channel tagging stakeholders + decision owner so the record
   survives turnover.

## Decision points

- **After Frame:** if the stakeholder re-frames the problem as "minor",
  restart with stronger evidence instead of pushing forward. A forced
  decision on a problem they do not own will not stick.
- **After Options:** if no option emerges and stakeholder asks for "more
  time", commit them to a specific decide-by time before the call ends.
  Open-ended deferral is how escalations die.
- **After Decide:** if the decision feels vague when you read it back, do
  not accept it. Rewrite live until both sides can name the next concrete
  action.
- **After Close:** if the stakeholder cannot name the follow-up date
  without checking their calendar, you have not landed the urgency.
  Re-anchor on the metric impact and the deadline.
