# Client onboarding into our delivery cadence (two weeks)

## Context

Take a newly signed client (or new stakeholder cohort on an existing account) from contract to confident participant in our weekly demo / report / decision loop with no surprises.

Tier: **pro**. Complexity: **medium**. Group: **role-project-manager**. Persona: **role-project-manager**.

## Outcome

This playbook is done when:

- Client maturity assessment captured.
- Onboarding 1-pager confirmed in writing.
- First full delivery cycle attended by client.
- Practice change request filed.
- Sponsor 'confident' verdict at week 2.

## Steps

### 1. Client maturity read

Assess how the client likes to work.

Tasks:
- Interview the client sponsor on tools, cadence, and decision style.
- Score the client on PM maturity rubric.
- Identify the most likely friction points.

Outputs:
- Client maturity assessment.

Decision gate: Advance when maturity is rated and friction list is on paper.

### 2. Onboarding 1-pager

Document our delivery cadence in their language.

Tasks:
- Draft a one-page 'how we work together' explainer.
- Walk it with the client sponsor.
- Adjust language for their tooling.

Outputs:
- Onboarding one-pager.

Decision gate: Advance when the client confirms understanding in writing.

### 3. Tool + access setup

Give the client real-time visibility.

Tasks:
- Provision client access to the board + dashboard.
- Configure read-only or contributor permissions.
- Test their access end-to-end.

Outputs:
- Confirmed tool access.

Decision gate: Advance when the client logs in and verifies they see the right data.

### 4. Run the first cadence

Walk through one full delivery week.

Tasks:
- Run a kickoff demo + first weekly report cycle.
- Hold one decision-loop meeting with the client.
- Collect feedback on the cadence.

Outputs:
- First-cycle artifacts (demo, report, decision).

Decision gate: Advance when the client engages with all three rituals.

### 5. Lock change-control

Set expectations around scope changes.

Tasks:
- Explain the change-control process with examples.
- Run one practice change request through the loop.
- Confirm the procurement/contract path for change orders.

Outputs:
- Change-control walkthrough record.

Decision gate: Advance when the client has filed at least one mock CR.

### 6. Two-week confidence check

Confirm the client can self-serve into our cadence.

Tasks:
- Survey the sponsor on confidence + clarity.
- Note any rituals they want changed.
- Lock the second-week cadence as the steady state.

Outputs:
- Confidence check note.

Decision gate: Required: a written 'confident' verdict from the client sponsor.

## Decision points

- Read-only vs contributor access — contributor only after the client demonstrates they understand the workflow.
- Adopt client tools vs require ours — adopt theirs for status visibility; keep ours for delivery execution.
