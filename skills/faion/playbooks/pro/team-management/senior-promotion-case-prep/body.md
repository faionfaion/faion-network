# Senior promotion case prep (every 6 months)

**Persona:** P4 Outsource Specialist · **Tier:** pro · **Complexity:** medium · **Angle:** atomic

## Why this playbook exists

Half-day reflective task before promotion cycle → written case mapping last 6 months at the shop to senior/lead competency rubric, with evidence reviewer can verify.

Atomic half-day ceremony repeated each promotion cycle. Output: a written case that maps the last 6 months to the senior/lead competency rubric, with evidence the reviewer can verify without you in the room. Used at outsource shops where promotion is calibrated against an internal rubric.

Most outsource seniors improvise this flow — fine at one engagement, costly across five. This playbook fixes the chain: explicit stages, explicit decision-gates, written artifacts at every step, AI agents on a leash, no silent work absorption.

## How to run it

Walk the stages in order. Each stage has a decision-gate; do not advance until the gate condition is met in writing. A stranger should be able to read the artifacts and understand both *what* you did and *why* you advanced. Atomic stages are designed to be completed in a single sitting; deep stages span multiple sessions.

## Stage map

### Stage 1 — Inventory + rubric mapping

**Intent:** Match what you did to what HR scores you on.

**Tasks**
- Pull the rubric from HR
- List engagements over the last 6 months
- Map evidence to each rubric line
- Build a 30-60-90 forward plan to show direction

**Outputs**
- Evidence matrix
- 30-60-90 forward plan

**Decision gate**

Advance only when every rubric line has evidence or an honest 'gap' note.

### Stage 2 — STAR stories

**Intent:** Tell 3 stories that survive a reviewer's stress-test.

**Tasks**
- Pick 3 high-impact moments
- Frame each in STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Apply storytelling patterns to keep them readable
- Lessons-learned style: what you'd do differently

**Outputs**
- 3 STAR stories

**Decision gate**

Advance only when stories land impact in numbers, not adjectives.

### Stage 3 — Peer feedback + filing

**Intent:** Triangulate the self-narrative with external voices.

**Tasks**
- Solicit feedback from ≥3 peers using a tested script
- Apply feedback patterns to integrate critique
- Compile final case
- File with HR + your manager before the deadline

**Outputs**
- Peer feedback collated
- Final case doc
- Filed with HR

**Decision gate**

Done when case is filed AND each peer quote is verifiable.

## Common pitfalls

- Self-narrative without peer triangulation — reviewer discounts it
- Adjectives instead of metrics — stories evaporate at panel review
- Filing without a forward plan — reviewers want direction, not just history

## Quality checklist

- Could a reviewer verify every claim without me in the room?
- Did I show a stretch goal, not just achievements?
- Did I solicit feedback from people who'd push back?
