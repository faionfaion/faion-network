# Newsletter to paid product funnel: convert 1K free readers into 200 customers

## Why this playbook exists
Indie hackers run the entire pipeline alone — research, build, ship, market, support, and decide — under tight calendar constraints and often from a nomad schedule. 1K free newsletter readers → 200 paying customers at $20 average ($4K MRR). This playbook turns that ambition into a sequenced set of stages with explicit decision gates so the founder doesn't confuse motion for progress and doesn't keep paying for products that quietly stopped working.

## What changes after running it
Engineer a documented funnel that turns a 1K-subscriber free newsletter into roughly 200 paying customers at $20 average through low-friction email-driven conversion, hitting $4K MRR with no paid traffic.

## Stage flow
### Audit
Know which issues, segments, and CTAs already convert.

**Tasks**
- Pull last 90 days of opens, clicks, and replies by issue
- Tag every CTA by intent and outcome
- Identify the 3 highest-converting topics

**Decision gate**
> Advance when CTA performance has been segmented and ranked.

### Offer
A paid offer tied to the top-converting topic.

**Tasks**
- Define the paid product framing (job + price + format)
- Write the offer page with proof and pricing
- Pick a single pricing hypothesis ($X for Y format)

**Decision gate**
> Advance when 3 readers describe the offer back unaided.

### Drip
An onboarding sequence makes the first paid pitch.

**Tasks**
- Write a 5-email onboarding sequence ending in CTA
- Insert in-issue ads in regular sends
- Tag click-to-CTA users for a follow-up nudge

**Decision gate**
> Advance when 3 paid customers from the drip have closed.

### Convert
Run paid pushes that respect the list.

**Tasks**
- Quarterly launch push (≤1 per quarter)
- Monthly soft promo woven into normal issues
- Personal outreach to engaged-non-buyers

**Decision gate**
> Advance when conversion rate stabilises ≥2% per push.

### Retain
Keep buyers paying month over month.

**Tasks**
- Send a paid-only weekly digest
- Trigger churn-save sequence on cancel intent
- Quarterly OKR review of LTV, churn, and ARPU

**Decision gate**
> Maintain only if 90-day churn ≤5%; otherwise re-spec offer.

## When to skip a stage
Every stage exists because indie hackers historically skip exactly that one — Validate is skipped by builders who fall in love with code, Decide is skipped by founders who can't bear to kill a product, and Cadence is skipped when travel tempts a creator into silence. If you must skip a stage, write a one-paragraph rationale in the playbook artefact log so the next run can audit the trade-off.

## How this playbook compounds
The artefacts from each run — quotes, postmortems, conversion tables, hook banks — feed directly into the next playbook the founder runs. A weekly review fed by these outputs is the difference between a portfolio of half-finished projects and a portfolio of compounding bets. Treat every gap[] entry as a methodology to ship; the playbook stays in draft until the gaps clear.

## Reading order
First-time runs should read the playbook YAML end-to-end before starting Stage 1. Repeat runs can jump to the stage matching current ambiguity and skim only that stage's methodologies. The decision gate is always the source of truth — even if the founder feels confident, the gate decides.

## Failure modes to watch
- Mistaking audience engagement for paying intent; the playbook insists on a paid checkpoint.
- Letting one stage drag because the next is intimidating — the gate is meant to force the move.
- Burning out by trying to combine this playbook with two others in the same week.

## Done means done
The playbook is done when every success criterion is checked AND a written verdict exists in the founder's notes. Anything short of a written verdict is an in-progress playbook — file it back into the queue and keep going.
