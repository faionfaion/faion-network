# Product Hunt launch campaign: 4-week prep to #1-of-day

## Why this playbook exists
Indie hackers run the entire pipeline alone — research, build, ship, market, support, and decide — under tight calendar constraints and often from a nomad schedule. Shipping product → top-3 Product Hunt launch with measurable signup and revenue lift. This playbook turns that ambition into a sequenced set of stages with explicit decision gates so the founder doesn't confuse motion for progress and doesn't keep paying for products that quietly stopped working.

## What changes after running it
Run a structured 4-week campaign that takes a shipping indie product to a top-3 Product Hunt launch, with a measurable signup and revenue lift, then converts the spike into retained subscribers and audience.

## Stage flow
### Asset Prep
Page, copy, and screenshots ready 14 days before launch.

**Tasks**
- Write Product Hunt copy aligned to positioning
- Generate gallery: hero, demo, problem-solved
- Stage the landing page for PH-referred traffic

**Decision gate**
> Advance when 3 outside readers say the listing makes them want to sign up.

### Audience Warm-up
Audience knows the launch date and committed to support.

**Tasks**
- Drip teaser content over X and the newsletter for 14 days
- DM 50 hunters and makers with personal asks
- Schedule a launch-eve nudge

**Decision gate**
> Advance when ≥30 makers confirm they will support on launch day.

### Launch Day
Top-3 by EOD via a tight runbook.

**Tasks**
- Publish at 00:01 PT; pin tweet; queue first 5 comments
- Reply to every comment within 30 minutes
- Push hourly progress updates in primary channel

**Decision gate**
> Stay in launch mode until midnight; advance when day-1 rank is locked.

### Conversion
Spike traffic converts to paid or trial users.

**Tasks**
- Activate onboarding email sequence for new signups
- Route hot leads to a personal welcome DM
- Run a 24-hour PH-day promo for paid plans

**Decision gate**
> Advance if day-7 paid conversion is ≥2% of new signups.

### Compounding
Launch spike feeds long-term audience growth.

**Tasks**
- Migrate visible new followers to newsletter via pinned tweet
- Publish a post-launch retro with numbers
- Add launch-week customers to a testimonial pipeline

**Decision gate**
> Advance only after numbers and testimonials are public.

## When to skip a stage
Every stage exists because indie hackers historically skip exactly that one — Validate is skipped by builders who fall in love with code, Decide is skipped by founders who can't bear to kill a product, and Cadence is skipped when travel tempts a creator into silence. If you must skip a stage, write a one-paragraph rationale in the playbook artefact log so the next run can audit the trade-off.

## How this playbook compounds
The artefacts from each run — quotes, postmortems, conversion tables, hook banks — feed directly into the next playbook the founder runs. A weekly review fed by these outputs is the difference between a portfolio of half-finished projects and a portfolio of compounding bets. Treat every gap[] entry as a methodology to ship; the playbook stays in draft until the gaps clear.

## Reading order
First-time runs should read the playbook YAML end-to-end before starting Stage 1. Repeat runs can jump to the stage matching current ambiguity and skim only that stage's methodologies. The decision gate is always the source of truth — even if the founder feels confident, the gate decides.

## Failure modes to watch
- Mistaking audience engagement for paying intent; the playbook insists on a paid checkpoint.
- Letting one stage drag because the next is intimidating — the gate is meant to force the move.
- Burning out by trying to combine this playbook with two others in the same week.

## Done means done
The playbook is done when every success criterion is checked AND a written verdict exists in the founder's notes. Anything short of a written verdict is an in-progress playbook — file it back into the queue and keep going.
