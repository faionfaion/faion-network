# Multi-product portfolio rotation: ship N small bets per year

## Why this playbook exists
Indie hackers run the entire pipeline alone — research, build, ship, market, support, and decide — under tight calendar constraints and often from a nomad schedule. Random side-projects → a yearly rotation of 4–6 small bets compounding into ramen profitability. This playbook turns that ambition into a sequenced set of stages with explicit decision gates so the founder doesn't confuse motion for progress and doesn't keep paying for products that quietly stopped working.

## What changes after running it
Solo indie hacker runs a documented quarterly rotation that ships 4 to 6 small products per year, keeps the 2 to 3 with traction, sunsets the rest, and compounds the survivors into a portfolio that hits ramen profitability.

## Stage flow
### Backlog
A live idea-pool feeds the rotation faster than ideas die.

**Tasks**
- Maintain an idea inbox tagged by JTBD and audience-fit
- Score new entries weekly using a lightweight RICE
- Cull the bottom quartile every month

**Decision gate**
> Advance when ≥10 ideas sit in the inbox with scores.

### Rotation
One bet per quarter enters build; one exits to keep / kill.

**Tasks**
- Lock the bet for the quarter — no scope-swap mid-cycle
- Define the kill criteria up front
- Schedule a 7-day spike to confirm feasibility

**Decision gate**
> Advance if spike confirms feasibility within the quarter's effort budget.

### Ship
Bet reaches MVP launch within the quarter.

**Tasks**
- Run the MVP launch checklist (positioning, pricing, payment)
- Open a Stripe path even for free-tier products
- Capture day-1 telemetry from launch

**Decision gate**
> Advance if MVP launches within the quarter; else move bet to 'aborted' with notes.

### Triage
Each quarter, survivors get sustained; losers get sunsetted.

**Tasks**
- Run portfolio scorecard at quarter-end
- Move survivors to maintain mode; sunset the rest
- Schedule sunset comms and asset harvest

**Decision gate**
> Each product: keep if revenue OR active-user metric trends up; sunset if neither.

### Compound
Surviving products share content, audience, and automation.

**Tasks**
- Cross-link audiences between survivors
- Consolidate content engine across products
- Automate operational toil with shared scripts

**Decision gate**
> Advance into the next rotation when surviving products run on <1 day/wk of toil.

## When to skip a stage
Every stage exists because indie hackers historically skip exactly that one — Validate is skipped by builders who fall in love with code, Decide is skipped by founders who can't bear to kill a product, and Cadence is skipped when travel tempts a creator into silence. If you must skip a stage, write a one-paragraph rationale in the playbook artefact log so the next run can audit the trade-off.

## How this playbook compounds
The artefacts from each run — quotes, postmortems, conversion tables, hook banks — feed directly into the next playbook the founder runs. A weekly review fed by these outputs is the difference between a portfolio of half-finished projects and a portfolio of compounding bets. Treat every gap[] entry as a methodology to ship; the playbook stays in draft until the gaps clear.

## Reading order
First-time runs should read the playbook YAML end-to-end before starting Stage 1. Repeat runs can jump to the stage matching current ambiguity and skim only that stage's methodologies. The decision gate is always the source of truth — even if the founder feels confident, the gate decides.

## Failure modes to watch
- Mistaking audience engagement for paying intent; the playbook insists on a paid checkpoint.
- Letting one stage drag because the next is intimidating — the gate is meant to force the move.
- Burning out by trying to combine this playbook with two others in the same week.

## Done means done
The playbook is done when every success criterion is checked AND a written verdict exists in the founder's notes. Anything short of a written verdict is an in-progress playbook — file it back into the queue and keep going.
