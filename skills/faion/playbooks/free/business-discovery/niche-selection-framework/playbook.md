## Goal

After this playbook you will have scored at least five candidate niches on four axes (Pain, Reachability, Willingness-to-Pay, Personal Fit), ranked them by total score, and committed in writing to one niche to pursue first — with a documented rationale for why the others were set aside.

## Prerequisites

- A rough problem space in mind (e.g. "something for freelancers", "a tool for parents worried about screen time").
- At least five candidate niches written out as a specific buyer description: one sentence each, naming who the person is and what context they are in. Generic categories like "small businesses" or "developers" do not count as niches here.
- A spreadsheet, Notion table, or plain text file open and ready to record scores.
- Optional but valuable: notes from 2–3 customer discovery conversations (see [mom-test-customer-interview](../mom-test-customer-interview/playbook.md)).

## Steps

1. Open your scoring sheet and create a table with these columns: `Niche`, `Pain (1-5)`, `Reachability (1-5)`, `WTP (1-5)`, `Personal Fit (1-5)`, `Total`, `Notes`.

2. Write your five candidate niches as one-sentence buyer descriptions. Use the pattern: `[who] + [doing what] + [in what context]`. Here are five real-sounding examples to fill in before you replace them with your own:
   - Freelance interpreters in Berlin who take back-to-back conference assignments and bill by the hour
   - Roblox parents wanting content safety filters for their 8–12 year olds
   - Solo accountants in Poland who handle VAT filings for 10–30 small business clients
   - Independent personal trainers who run 1-on-1 sessions and chase payments manually
   - Micro-brewery owners (< 5 staff) managing tap-room shifts without dedicated POS software

3. Score each niche on **Pain (1-5)**. Ask: does this person have a recurring, urgent, expensive, or embarrassing problem they are actively trying to solve right now? Score guide:
   - 5 = urgent pain, person already spending money or time on workarounds
   - 3 = annoying but manageable; they complain but do not act
   - 1 = no clear pain; the problem is theoretical

4. Score each niche on **Reachability (1-5)**. Ask: can you find and talk to 20 of these people in the next two weeks without a budget? Score guide:
   - 5 = dense, searchable community (LinkedIn group, Slack workspace, subreddit, trade show, local meetup)
   - 3 = reachable but slow; requires cold outreach or referral chains
   - 1 = impossible to find in bulk; no obvious gathering point, no shared identity

5. Score each niche on **Willingness-to-Pay (1-5)**. Ask: does this person already pay for tools or services that solve adjacent problems? Score guide:
   - 5 = active buyer — has a budget line, pays for multiple SaaS tools, or pays professionals for this type of work
   - 3 = occasional buyer — has paid once or twice but default is free tools
   - 1 = never pays — student, hobbyist, or fully covered by employer

6. Score each niche on **Personal Fit (1-5)**. Ask: do you have direct experience with this person's world, existing credibility, or genuine curiosity that will sustain you through 50 customer conversations? Score guide:
   - 5 = you have lived this problem, worked in this industry, or know 10+ people in this niche personally
   - 3 = adjacent — you understand the domain but are not a native
   - 1 = complete outsider; you chose this niche because it "sounds big"

7. Sum the four scores for each niche. Add a short `Notes` entry explaining one thing you learned or assumed about that niche during scoring.

8. **Worked example** — apply the scorecard to the five sample niches above:

   | Niche | Pain | Reach | WTP | Fit | Total |
   |-------|------|-------|-----|-----|-------|
   | Freelance interpreters in Berlin | 4 | 3 | 4 | 2 | **13** |
   | Roblox parents / safety filters | 3 | 4 | 3 | 4 | **14** |
   | Solo accountants (Poland, VAT) | 5 | 3 | 5 | 3 | **16** |
   | Independent personal trainers | 4 | 4 | 3 | 3 | **14** |
   | Micro-brewery owners | 3 | 2 | 3 | 2 | **10** |

   In this example, **solo accountants** win on Pain and WTP (they already pay for compliance tools and the cost of a VAT error is high), but the builder scored Personal Fit at 3 because they have a family member who is an accountant — adjacent, not native. The micro-brewery niche loses on Reachability and WTP; there is no critical mass of brewery owners in one online channel, and many run on thin margins. Roblox parents scored 14 — tied with personal trainers — but the builder gave parents higher Personal Fit (4) because they are a parent themselves and already active in parenting communities on Reddit.

9. Replace the sample niches with your own five candidates and run the same scoring. Do not adjust your scores retroactively to make a preferred niche win.

10. Pick the highest-scoring niche. If two niches are within 1 point of each other, default to the one with the higher Personal Fit score — because early-stage traction runs on founder energy and access, not pure market size.

11. Write one paragraph in your notes file: the niche you chose, the score, and a one-sentence explanation of why each of the other four niches was set aside. This is your commitment document. If you revisit this decision later, update it — do not delete it.

## Verify

Open your scoring sheet. You have completed this playbook if:

- All five niches have scores in all four columns (no blanks).
- Exactly one niche is marked as "selected" with a written rationale.
- The Notes column has at least one sentence per niche.

If two or more niches are marked "selected", you have not made a decision — pick the one with higher Personal Fit and archive the rest.

## Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Every niche scores 3 on Pain | You are scoring your assumptions, not evidence | Run at least two [mom-test-customer-interview](../mom-test-customer-interview/playbook.md) sessions before scoring; replace assumed pain with observed pain from conversations |
| All five niches score low on Reachability | Your niches are defined too broadly or too narrowly | Rewrite each niche description to include a gathering point: "freelance translators who post in ProZ.com forums" scores higher than "translators" because you can find them |
| The top-scoring niche feels wrong | Personal Fit is masking a weak Pain or WTP score | Re-read your Pain and WTP scores independently. A high Personal Fit should amplify a real Pain, not substitute for it; if Pain is below 3, no amount of access or enthusiasm rescues the niche |
| You cannot think of five niches | Your problem space is too vague | Start from the most specific buyer you can imagine — "my coworker Sarah who does X" — then list four adjacent variations who have the same problem |
| Two niches tie and you cannot choose | Both are genuinely viable | Pick the one you can reach faster (higher Reachability); the goal is to start customer conversations this week, not to optimise the decision forever |
