# Annual delivery-process maturity review

## Context

Once a year (or semi-annually for high-growth orgs), audit our delivery practice across all active projects, identify top systemic friction, and ship a concrete improvement program for the next cycle.

Tier: **geek**. Complexity: **deep**. Group: **role-project-manager**. Persona: **role-project-manager**.

## Outcome

This playbook is done when:

- Portfolio rollup covers 100% of active projects.
- Top-5 systemic friction list with cost.
- Maturity self-assessment challenged by reviewer.
- Improvement program with owners + budget.
- Mid-cycle review delivered at month 6.

## Steps

### 1. Portfolio rollup

See the whole delivery picture before judging.

Tasks:
- Roll up EVM across all active projects.
- Pull velocity + risk + lessons aggregates.
- Map active projects on a complexity × maturity grid.

Outputs:
- Portfolio rollup deck.

Decision gate: Advance when the rollup covers 100% of active projects.

### 2. Friction inventory

List the friction that recurs across projects.

Tasks:
- Cluster lessons-learned across the year.
- Identify top 5 systemic friction patterns.
- Tag each as people / process / tooling.

Outputs:
- Top-5 systemic friction list.

Decision gate: Advance when each friction pattern is named with frequency + cost.

### 3. Maturity rating

Rate ourselves against a delivery maturity rubric.

Tasks:
- Apply the PMI performance-domains lens.
- Self-rate each domain on a 1-5 maturity scale.
- Get one independent reviewer to challenge the ratings.

Outputs:
- Maturity self-assessment.

Decision gate: Advance when ratings are challenged and revised.

### 4. AI-leverage scan

Audit where AI tooling could shift the curve.

Tasks:
- List PM tasks currently consuming the most time.
- Score each for AI augmentation potential.
- Identify the top 3 candidate workflows.

Outputs:
- AI-leverage opportunity list.

Decision gate: Advance when 3 candidate workflows are named with effort × payoff.

### 5. Improvement program

Convert friction + AI opportunities into a 6-12 month plan.

Tasks:
- Pick the top 3 friction items to attack.
- Pick the top 1-2 AI experiments.
- Sequence them with named owners + budget.

Outputs:
- Improvement program plan.

Decision gate: Advance when the plan has owners + budget + start dates.

### 6. Communicate + commit

Ship the plan publicly inside the org.

Tasks:
- Present the maturity review to leadership.
- Publish a one-page summary to the delivery org.
- Schedule a mid-cycle progress checkpoint.

Outputs:
- Published maturity review summary.

Decision gate: Advance when leadership signs off on the program.

### 7. Mid-cycle checkpoint

Watch the improvement program execute.

Tasks:
- Hold a mid-cycle review against named milestones.
- Adjust the program based on what is and is not working.
- Document interim wins + setbacks.

Outputs:
- Mid-cycle review note.

Decision gate: Required: a written mid-cycle check at month 6.

## Decision points

- Process-fix vs tooling-fix — fix the process first; tooling without process change just accelerates the mess.
- AI experiment vs proven workflow — limit AI experiments to 1-2 in parallel to keep them measurable.
