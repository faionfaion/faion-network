# Pricing Strategy

## Summary

A value-based framework for setting prices: calculate cost floor, estimate customer value
ceiling, research willingness to pay, choose a pricing model, and test. Price should be
10–20% of the measurable value delivered — not cost-plus or competitor-copy.

## Why

Pricing determines business model, customer expectations, and growth trajectory. Cost-plus
pricing systematically underprices; competitor-copying ignores value differentiation.
Value-based pricing with Van Westendorp research finds the acceptable range empirically,
then psychological anchoring maximizes revenue within it.

## When To Use

- Launching a new product and setting initial price for the first time.
- Conversion rate is below 1% and price objections appear in sales calls.
- Conversion rate is above 5% — signal that price is too low.
- Redesigning tier structure (too many tiers, wrong feature allocation).
- Quarterly pricing review to adjust for market changes.

## When NOT To Use

- Pre-product-market-fit stage — pricing experiments are meaningless without retention signal.
- Regulated industries where price is externally constrained.
- Marketplace products where pricing is set by supply/demand dynamics.
- When the real blocker is positioning, not price — fix messaging first.
