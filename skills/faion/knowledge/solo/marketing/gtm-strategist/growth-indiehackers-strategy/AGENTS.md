# IndieHackers Build-in-Public Strategy

## Summary

**One-sentence:** A give-first community strategy for IndieHackers: spend 2–4 weeks helping others before any self-promotion, then build an audience through transparent build-in-public updates (monthly revenue reports, wins, losses).

**One-paragraph:** A give-first community strategy for IndieHackers: spend 2–4 weeks helping others before any self-promotion, then build an audience through transparent build-in-public updates (monthly revenue reports, wins, losses). Community converts to customers only after you are recognized as a genuine contributor — not a promoter.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Building a product for other solopreneurs, indie hackers, or bootstrappers.
- Need early adopters and peer feedback from an experienced builder community.
- Product has a build-in-public story to tell (transparent revenue milestones).
- Looking for accountability partners, collaborators, or co-marketing.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- B2B enterprise or corporate-audience products — wrong community.
- Need customers immediately — IH requires 4–8 weeks before any promotion yields results.
- Product has nothing to do with building/running a business.
- Unwilling to share revenue numbers — transparency is the community norm; obscuring metrics reads as inauthentic.
