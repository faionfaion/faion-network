# Example

I almost quit last year.

[blank line]

My startup was failing. Revenue was down 40%.
I had 3 months of runway left.

Here's what I learned:

1. Panic decisions are always wrong.
The urge to pivot immediately was the biggest threat — not the revenue drop.

2. Your best customers will tell you exactly how to survive.
Three calls with churned customers gave me more clarity than 3 months of internal meetings.

3. "Running out of money" and "the idea is wrong" are not the same thing.
One is a distribution problem. The other is a product problem. Know which one you have.

Today we're profitable and growing.

That crisis was the best thing that could have happened.

What's a "failure" that taught you something you still use today?
