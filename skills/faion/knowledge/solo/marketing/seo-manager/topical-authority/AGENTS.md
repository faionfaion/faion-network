# Topical Authority

## Summary

Build topical authority by covering an entire subject comprehensively through topic clusters: one pillar page (broad overview) plus supporting articles (subtopics), all internally linked. Google's ranking algorithm rewards sites that demonstrate comprehensive domain expertise over those targeting isolated keywords.

## Why

Keyword density stopped correlating with rankings when Google introduced semantic search and BERT/MUM. The algorithm now assesses whether a site is a credible, comprehensive source on a topic. A site with 30 tightly linked articles on RAG outranks a site with one article optimized for "RAG tutorial" — because the cluster signals genuine expertise rather than keyword targeting.

## When To Use

- Planning a new content area and wanting to establish authority quickly
- Existing content exists but rankings have plateaued despite on-page optimization
- Building a pillar-cluster content strategy from scratch
- Evaluating which topics to invest in based on authority-building ROI

## When NOT To Use

- Single-page or micro-sites with no room for content clusters
- When the bottleneck is technical SEO, not content depth — fix crawlability first
- For time-sensitive news/event content where topical depth is not the ranking factor
