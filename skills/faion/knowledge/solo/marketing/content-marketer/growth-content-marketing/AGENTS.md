# Content Marketing

## Summary

A systematic framework for generating consistent organic traffic through content: define pillars, research keywords, create content, distribute, measure, iterate. The core rule: spend 50% of total content production time on distribution — publishing without distribution is the primary reason content marketing fails to produce traffic.

## Why

Content marketing is the only paid-acquisition alternative that compounds over time: a well-ranked blog post generates traffic for years at zero marginal cost. Most content marketing fails not from poor writing but from random publishing (no pillar strategy), writing for the wrong audience, or treating SEO as an afterthought. A structured pillar + keyword intent + distribution checklist approach cuts time-to-ranking by removing guesswork.

## When To Use

- Generating keyword research lists and content briefs for a defined topic cluster
- Drafting long-form blog posts from a structured brief (keyword, intent, outline, competitors)
- Building a 3-month content calendar with topics mapped to funnel stages
- Repurposing a long-form piece into 5-7 derivative formats (tweet thread, newsletter, LinkedIn, carousel)
- Writing distribution checklists and outreach messages for newly published content

## When NOT To Use

- Defining content pillars — requires ICP interviews and product positioning work, not synthesis
- Content that depends on original data, proprietary research, or lived experience
- Publishing directly to any CMS without human review
- Predicting SEO performance — agents cannot query live search engine data
- Replacing a content strategist's judgment on topic prioritization for a specific brand
