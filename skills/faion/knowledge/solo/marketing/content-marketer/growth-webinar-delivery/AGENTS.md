# Webinar Delivery and Follow-up

## Summary

The execution half of webinars: deliver an engaging live session (polls every 10 minutes,
energy higher than normal, camera-at-eye level), convert attendees via a time-limited offer
during Q&A, then run a 5-email follow-up sequence over 5 days. Repurpose the recording into
10+ assets (blog post, YouTube, podcast, social clips, quote graphics).

## Why

Most webinars convert below 3% because hosts treat them as one-way presentations and send
a single "replay available" email. The mechanism: live engagement tactics (polls, exercises)
hold attention and create reciprocity; a scarcity-framed offer during Q&A converts while
intent is highest; the 5-email sequence converts late-deciders who need one more nudge;
repurposing multiplies the content ROI 10x from a single production effort.

## When To Use

- Running a live educational webinar with a product offer at the end.
- Post-webinar follow-up sequence is not set up or is a single email.
- Webinar recordings are sitting unused — need a repurposing workflow.
- Show-up rate is above 30% but conversion is below 5%.

## When NOT To Use

- Webinar is not recorded — repurposing workflow requires recording.
- Product offer is not ready — do not run a conversion sequence without a concrete offer and deadline.
- Audience is too small (fewer than 20 registrants) — low absolute conversion numbers make optimization difficult.
- Demo/product webinar for enterprise sales — different format (discovery + qualification), not conversion webinar.
