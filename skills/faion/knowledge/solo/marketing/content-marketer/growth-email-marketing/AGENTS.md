# Email Marketing

## Summary

A value-first email marketing framework covering list building, welcome sequences, newsletter strategy, segmentation, and sales email sequences. The core rule: never purchase or rent lists — spam complaints above 0.1% trigger Google/Yahoo bulk sender sanctions, and deliverability damage is difficult to reverse. Build organically with lead magnets; automate the welcome sequence before any broadcast.

## Why

Email is the only audience channel you fully own — social algorithms can eliminate your reach overnight. But most email marketing fails because it prioritizes promotion over value (80% value, 20% pitch is the standard), sends irregularly, or skips the welcome sequence that captures subscribers at peak engagement. A structured Build→Nurture→Segment→Convert→Retain flow consistently produces 20-30%+ open rates where ad-driven lists produce single digits.

## When To Use

- Launching a new product and building an email list from scratch
- Setting up automated welcome and nurture sequences for an existing audience
- Converting social media followers to an owned channel
- Running a promotional campaign (product launch, sale, cohort open)
- Re-engaging an inactive or cold subscriber list

## When NOT To Use

- Audience is still undefined — list-building without ICP clarity creates churn
- Fewer than 50 subscribers with no consistent content cadence — focus on content first
- Pure B2B enterprise with procurement-driven sales — email alone won't close
- Compliance requirements prevent automated outreach (certain financial or healthcare contexts)
