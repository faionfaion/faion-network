# Customer Testimonials and Social Proof

## Summary

A systematic process for collecting, strengthening, and placing customer testimonials
to reduce purchase friction. The methodology covers segment-to-placement mapping,
structured ask sequences, quote transformation, and a social proof hierarchy.
The core rule: ask at the moment of peak satisfaction (immediately after a measurable
win), never at a random interval.

## Why

Prospects discount vendor marketing claims. Testimonials from real customers with
specific results ("40% signup lift in 30 days") provide the social proof that converts
skeptics. Generic quotes ("great product!") have near-zero conversion impact; result-
specific quotes with full attribution (name, role, company, photo) measurably lift CTR
and conversion on landing pages.

## When To Use

- Landing page conversion rate is below benchmark and social proof is absent or weak
- Launching a new product to systematically build the first testimonial bank
- Preparing a case study library for a sales or marketing campaign
- Setting up post-purchase automation to collect testimonials at scale
- Redesigning a landing page where testimonials are generic or buried

## When NOT To Use

- No existing customers yet — fabricated or placeholder testimonials are worse than none
- Regulated industry requiring legal review of customer quotes before publication
- Customer relationships under 2 weeks old — asking too early damages trust
- B2B enterprise where case studies require approval chains spanning months — use
  logo wall + usage stats instead
