# Customer Testimonials and Social Proof

## Summary

**One-sentence:** A systematic process for collecting, strengthening, and placing customer testimonials to reduce purchase friction.

**One-paragraph:** A systematic process for collecting, strengthening, and placing customer testimonials to reduce purchase friction. The methodology covers segment-to-placement mapping, structured ask sequences, quote transformation, and a social proof hierarchy. The core rule: ask at the moment of peak satisfaction (immediately after a measurable win), never at a random interval.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Landing page conversion rate is below benchmark and social proof is absent or weak
- Launching a new product to systematically build the first testimonial bank
- Preparing a case study library for a sales or marketing campaign
- Setting up post-purchase automation to collect testimonials at scale
- Redesigning a landing page where testimonials are generic or buried

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- No existing customers yet — fabricated or placeholder testimonials are worse than none
- Regulated industry requiring legal review of customer quotes before publication
- Customer relationships under 2 weeks old — asking too early damages trust
- B2B enterprise where case studies require approval chains spanning months — use logo wall + usage stats instead
