# Shutdown Customer Email Pack

## Summary

**One-sentence:** A three-email lifecycle (announce → refund/migrate → final thanks) for shutting down a paid indie product so customers learn early, get money back or a migration path, and leave with a positive last impression.

**One-paragraph:** Sunsetting a paid product is a recurring indie task and yet every founder writes the announcement from scratch under emotional pressure, usually too late and usually skipping refunds. This pack codifies the three emails every shutdown needs, with required content slots, a minimum 30-day notice window, and a "no silent disappearance" rule. The goal is not to save the failed product but to preserve the founder's future trust capital — customers who get a clean shutdown will buy from you next time.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- you are sunsetting a paid (subscription or one-time) product with real paying customers on file
- you have a working email channel to those customers (not just a Twitter follower count)
- the shutdown decision is firm — not a "maybe pivot" trial balloon
- tier == solo or higher (gating enforced by tier-manifest)

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- the product had zero paying customers (a blog post suffices)
- legal counsel has provided a tailored notice — defer to legal
- the product is being acquired and the buyer is sending its own comms
