# Ideation Methods: SCAMPER, Mind Mapping, and Starbursting

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Structured frameworks for generating and developing ideas systematically: SCAMPER (apply 7 lenses to an existing product to generate variants), Mind Mapping (visual branching for exploring connections), and Starbursting (5W+H question generation before answering).

**One-paragraph:** Structured frameworks for generating and developing ideas systematically: SCAMPER (apply 7 lenses to an existing product to generate variants), Mind Mapping (visual branching for exploring connections), and Starbursting (5W+H question generation before answering). Unlike group brainstorming techniques, these methods work solo or in small groups and produce structured idea artifacts, not just lists.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Improving or extending an existing product, service, or process (SCAMPER).
- Exploring a complex topic to map its structure and connections (Mind Mapping).
- Starting a new project where unknowns must be surfaced before planning (Starbursting).
- Preparing for a decision by identifying what you don't yet know.
- Risk analysis and pre-mortem exercises.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- When speed matters more than coverage — these methods take 30-90 minutes; use quick brainstorming instead.
- When the solution space is already well-defined — apply evaluation frameworks, not ideation ones.
- For emotional or relational issues — structured creativity tools are inappropriate in conflict contexts.
- When the group is too large (greater than 6) — use group brainstorming techniques instead.
