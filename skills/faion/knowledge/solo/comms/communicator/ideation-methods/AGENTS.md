# Ideation Methods

## Summary

Structured frameworks for generating and developing ideas systematically: SCAMPER (apply 7 lenses to an existing product to generate variants), Mind Mapping (visual branching for exploring connections), and Starbursting (5W+H question generation before answering). Unlike group brainstorming techniques, these methods work solo or in small groups and produce structured idea artifacts, not just lists.

## Why

Unstructured ideation produces ideas that cluster around the obvious. SCAMPER forces exploration of seven distinct transformation types (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse), systematically breaking cognitive fixation. Starbursting delays solution-jumping by generating questions first — the primary cause of failed product launches is insufficient question-asking before committing to an answer.

## When To Use

- Improving or extending an existing product, service, or process (SCAMPER)
- Exploring a complex topic to map its structure and connections (Mind Mapping)
- Starting a new project where unknowns must be surfaced before planning (Starbursting)
- Preparing for a decision by identifying what you don't yet know
- Risk analysis and pre-mortem exercises

## When NOT To Use

- When speed matters more than coverage — these methods take 30-90 minutes; use quick brainstorming instead
- When the solution space is already well-defined — apply evaluation frameworks, not ideation ones
- For emotional or relational issues — structured creativity tools are inappropriate in conflict contexts
- When the group is too large (&gt;6) — use group brainstorming techniques instead
