# Selling Ideas

## Summary

A persuasion framework for pitching technical or business ideas to different audiences. Combines SPIN Selling (uncover and amplify pain before presenting a solution), the Challenger Sale (teach a new insight, tailor, take control), the elevator pitch template, and the LAER objection-handling model. The core rule: never pitch until the audience feels the problem.

## Why

Great ideas fail because of bad pitches. Technical people default to feature explanations instead of problem-then-benefit narratives. SPIN research (Rackham, 35,000 sales calls) shows that implication questions — not product descriptions — are what move decision-makers. Matching pitch structure to audience role (executive vs technical vs finance) doubles comprehension and acceptance rates.

## When To Use

- Proposing a technical change, architecture decision, or new tooling to stakeholders
- Pitching a product idea to investors, clients, or executives
- Preparing for a presentation where you need a decision, not just understanding
- Writing a one-pager or executive summary that must persuade

## When NOT To Use

- When the audience has already decided — objection handling mode, not pitching mode
- Exploratory conversations where no decision is needed yet — use active-listening instead
- When you lack numbers or evidence — pitching without proof reads as speculation
- Pure technical briefings where the audience wants depth, not persuasion structure
