# Selling Ideas

## Summary

**One-sentence:** A persuasion framework for pitching technical or business ideas to different audiences.

**One-paragraph:** A persuasion framework for pitching technical or business ideas to different audiences. Combines SPIN Selling (uncover and amplify pain before presenting a solution), the Challenger Sale (teach a new insight, tailor, take control), the elevator pitch template, and the LAER objection-handling model. The core rule: never pitch until the audience feels the problem.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Proposing a technical change, architecture decision, or new tooling to stakeholders
- Pitching a product idea to investors, clients, or executives
- Preparing for a presentation where you need a decision, not just understanding
- Writing a one-pager or executive summary that must persuade

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- When the audience has already decided — objection handling mode, not pitching mode
- Exploratory conversations where no decision is needed yet — use active-listening instead
- When you lack numbers or evidence — pitching without proof reads as speculation
- Pure technical briefings where the audience wants depth, not persuasion structure
