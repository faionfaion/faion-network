# Business Storytelling

## Summary

Frameworks for structuring persuasive business communication so that audiences grasp the point immediately and remember it. The three primary structures are: Pyramid Principle (lead with answer, support with MECE arguments), SCQA (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer for narrative tension), and Pixar (causal "because of that" chain for change stories). Core rule: answer first, explain second — busy audiences stop reading the moment their question is answered.

## Why

Data alone does not persuade. Studies show people remember stories 22x better than facts. Audiences form judgments in the first 30 seconds; poorly structured presentations lose them before the key point lands. Pyramid Principle structures are the fastest way to ensure decision-makers receive the recommendation before the rationale — the opposite of how most technical people naturally communicate.

## When To Use

- Executive summaries, proposals, or recommendation memos
- Presentations where a decision is the outcome
- Case studies and success stories
- Change initiative communications where resistance is expected
- Any written communication longer than 3 paragraphs that needs to persuade

## When NOT To Use

- Exploratory or discovery sessions — narrative tension is not needed when gathering information
- Technical documentation where completeness matters more than persuasion
- Casual updates where no decision is required
- Data reports that are consumed by analysts who want raw structure, not narrative
