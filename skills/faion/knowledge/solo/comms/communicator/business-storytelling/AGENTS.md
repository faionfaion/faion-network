# Business Storytelling

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Frameworks for structuring persuasive business communication so that audiences grasp the point immediately and remember it.

**One-paragraph:** Frameworks for structuring persuasive business communication so that audiences grasp the point immediately and remember it. The three primary structures are: Pyramid Principle (lead with answer, support with MECE arguments), SCQA (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer for narrative tension), and Pixar (causal "because of that" chain for change stories). Core rule: answer first, explain second — busy audiences stop reading the moment their question is answered.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Executive summaries, proposals, or recommendation memos
- Presentations where a decision is the outcome
- Case studies and success stories
- Change initiative communications where resistance is expected
- Any written communication longer than 3 paragraphs that needs to persuade

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- Exploratory or discovery sessions — narrative tension is not needed when gathering information
- Technical documentation where completeness matters more than persuasion
- Casual updates where no decision is required
- Data reports that are consumed by analysts who want raw structure, not narrative
