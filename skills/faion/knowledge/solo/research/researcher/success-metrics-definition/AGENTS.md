# Success Metrics Definition

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Teams measure the wrong things or too many things.

**One-paragraph:** Teams measure the wrong things or too many things. Common issues: vanity metrics that don't drive decisions, too many KPIs with no focus, metrics that don't connect to business outcomes, and no targets or baselines. The root cause is having no structured process for identifying what truly matters. Success metrics are quantifiable measures that indicate whether you're achieving your goals. They answer: How do we know if we're winning? This methodology guides you through identifying what truly matters via a structured process that connects business goals to the AARRR framework, sets actionable targets, and guards against vanity metrics.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Setting up KPIs for a new product, feature, or campaign before launch.
- Re-baselining metrics quarterly when business goals shift.
- Onboarding a new team and aligning on a single North Star.
- After noticing the team optimizes for vanity numbers like signups or page views.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- During exploratory pre-PMF work — set qualitative milestones, not KPIs.
- For one-off experiments — use a single success criterion, not a full AARRR scaffold.
- When data infrastructure is missing; defining metrics without instrumentation creates aspirational dashboards.
- For purely creative/editorial work where the metric becomes a distorting incentive.
