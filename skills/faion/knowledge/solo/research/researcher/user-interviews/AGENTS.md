# User Interviews

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Founders skip user interviews or conduct them poorly.

**One-paragraph:** Founders skip user interviews or conduct them poorly. Common issues: asking leading questions, pitching instead of listening, accepting "I would" instead of "I did", not reaching enough people, and lacking systematic analysis of findings. User interviews are structured conversations with target customers to understand their problems, behaviors, and needs. Apply the Mom Test principles: talk about their life not your idea, ask about specifics in the past not generics about the future, and talk less and listen more.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Pre-MVP discovery to surface latent jobs, pains, and current solutions before committing to build.
- After launch when quantitative metrics show drop-off but root cause is unclear from data alone.
- Persona refinement and segmentation work to refine who your customers actually are.
- Pricing and packaging research when willingness to pay is unknown or varies by segment.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- For statistically significant claims. Interviews surface themes and patterns, not percentages. Use surveys after themes stabilize for larger sample.
- When you can already observe behavior via analytics or session recordings. Behavioral data trumps stated experience.
- For commodity or well-understood markets where interviews will repeat existing knowledge.
- As a substitute for behavioral validation. Interviews don't prove anyone will pay. They expose problems.
