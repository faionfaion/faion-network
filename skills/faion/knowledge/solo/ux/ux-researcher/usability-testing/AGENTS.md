# Usability Testing

## Summary

**One-sentence:** A structured method for observing real users attempt predefined tasks with a product.

**One-paragraph:** A structured method for observing real users attempt predefined tasks with a product. Sessions measure effectiveness (task completion), efficiency (time on task), and satisfaction (post-task ratings). Findings are prioritized by severity (Critical/High/Medium/Low) and reported with direct recommendations. Minimum 5 participants per segment for pattern detection.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Before a major feature launch — validate that task flows work for real users.
- When conversion or activation metrics are low with no clear analytics explanation.
- When redesigning navigation or an information architecture — test with think-aloud protocol.
- After implementing a competitor's pattern — verify it works for your users, not just theirs.
- When stakeholders disagree on a design direction — let test results arbitrate.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- When you need statistical significance across hundreds of users — use unmoderated studies with larger samples or quantitative A/B testing instead.
- When the question is "what should we build?" — use generative research (interviews, diary studies) before usability testing.
- On a prototype fidelity too low to support realistic tasks — results reflect prototype gaps, not product usability.
