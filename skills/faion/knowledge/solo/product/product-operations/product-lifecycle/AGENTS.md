# Product Lifecycle Management

## Summary

Framework for assessing which of four lifecycle stages a product is in (Introduction, Growth, Maturity, Decline) and applying stage-appropriate strategy, metrics, and investments. Assessment requires at least 6 quarters of revenue/growth/profitability/churn data. The key rule: strategy must match stage — growth tactics on a declining product and maturity investments in an early product both waste resources.

## Why

Products managed the same way regardless of maturity stage misallocate resources: growth spend on a declining product accelerates the burn, efficiency focus on an early-stage product starves learning. The lifecycle lens provides a vocabulary for portfolio decisions — which products to invest in, optimize, or sunset — and triggers transition planning a quarter before metrics shift so capability gaps (team mix, infra, KPIs) are filled in time.

## When To Use

- Annual planning: assess each product/SKU stage, reallocate investment.
- Multi-product portfolio (3+ products): agent normalizes stage assessment when one PM can't intuit all.
- Pre-sunset decision: building a defensible "stage = decline" case before EOL announcement.
- Spinning out a product line: confirm growth-stage signals warrant independent team.
- Acquisition due diligence: independent stage assessment versus seller's claim.

## When NOT To Use

- Single-product solo founder still finding product-market fit — entire roadmap is Introduction; lifecycle adds no signal.
- Quarter-by-quarter operations — lifecycle changes on multi-quarter horizons; tactical decisions don't need this lens.
- Heavily-regulated products with mandated lifecycles (medical devices) — formal regulatory framework supersedes.
