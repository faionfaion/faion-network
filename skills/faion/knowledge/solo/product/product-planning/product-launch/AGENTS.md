# Product Launch

## Summary

**One-sentence:** A product launch is a coordinated introduction of a product to its target market — not just a deployment, but a strategic moment to generate awareness and initial adoption.

**One-paragraph:** A product launch is a coordinated introduction of a product to its target market — not just a deployment, but a strategic moment to generate awareness and initial adoption. Planning starts 6–8 weeks out. Four components must be ready: positioning and messaging, assets (landing page, demo, docs), channels (email, social, communities), and coordination (internal team readiness, external partners).

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Shipping a new product or major version that targets a new or expanded audience.
- Any launch where first-week metrics (signups, activations, media mentions) matter.
- Coordinating multiple channels and teams (marketing, engineering, support) toward a common date.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- Internal releases or feature flags visible only to existing users — a changelog entry suffices.
- Bug fixes or performance improvements with no new user-facing surface.
- Continuous rollouts to a known user base — use a release notes workflow instead.
