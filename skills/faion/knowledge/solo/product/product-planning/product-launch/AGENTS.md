# Product Launch

## Summary

A product launch is a coordinated introduction of a product to its target market — not just a deployment, but a strategic moment to generate awareness and initial adoption. Planning starts 6–8 weeks out. Four components must be ready: positioning and messaging, assets (landing page, demo, docs), channels (email, social, communities), and coordination (internal team readiness, external partners).

## Why

"Just put it out there" launches produce a brief spike that disappears because no pre-launch audience was built, no messaging was aligned, and no one was ready to respond. A structured launch plan converts the deployment event into sustained user acquisition momentum.

## When To Use

- Shipping a new product or major version that targets a new or expanded audience.
- Any launch where first-week metrics (signups, activations, media mentions) matter.
- Coordinating multiple channels and teams (marketing, engineering, support) toward a common date.

## When NOT To Use

- Internal releases or feature flags visible only to existing users — a changelog entry suffices.
- Bug fixes or performance improvements with no new user-facing surface.
- Continuous rollouts to a known user base — use a release notes workflow instead.
