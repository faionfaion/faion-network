# Outcome-Based Roadmaps (Advanced)

## Summary

Advanced outcome roadmapping closes the gap between high-level business goals and the product experiments that move them. The key skill: decompose a business goal (e.g., "increase revenue 20%") into product outcomes (e.g., "reduce churn from 8% to 5%"), then into leading indicators and experiments. Use confidence levels instead of commitments, and tailor roadmap artifacts to each audience (customers, board, engineering, sales).

## Why

Stakeholders who receive feature timelines will always hold the team to dates. Stakeholders who receive outcome metrics accept a range of solutions and hold the team to results instead. The shift from "feature owner" to "outcome architect" is the most critical PM skill evolution for 2026 — driven by board-level expectation that product teams own revenue and profitability metrics, not just delivery velocity.

## When To Use

- Roadmap pressure from stakeholders who demand features and dates despite outcome focus.
- Decomposing a company OKR into product experiments that could plausibly move it.
- Quarterly roadmap presentations to different audiences (board, customers, engineering).
- After establishing basic roadmap-design — this extends it with outcome decomposition and audience segmentation.

## When NOT To Use

- Pre-PMF where there are no product outcomes to track yet — too early for decomposition.
- Compliance or operational work where outcomes are dictated, not hypothesized.
- Teams without any analytics instrumentation — outcome roadmaps require measurable leading indicators.
