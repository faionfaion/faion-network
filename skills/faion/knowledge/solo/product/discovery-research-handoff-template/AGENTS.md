# Discovery Research Handoff Template

## Summary

PMs run discovery (interviews, analytics dives, support-ticket trawls, competitor scans) and then hand the output to design and engineering. The handoff usually arrives as a Notion page, a Slack thread, or a slide deck — three formats, four lengths, zero standardisation. Designers re-derive personas, engineers ask "what's the success metric?" two sprints in, and discovery findings get re-discovered. This methodology defines a single one-page handoff artefact with five required sections (problem, target user, jobs-to-be-done, evidence, success metric) and the rule that no design or eng work starts before the handoff exists.

## Applies If

- A discovery cycle is producing findings that will feed downstream design or engineering work.
- The PM hands work to at least one separate function (design, engineering, marketing, partner) before shipping.
- The team can enforce a gate that says "no design jam until the handoff is published".
- The findings will inform commitments that span >1 sprint or affect roadmap priorities.

## Skip If

- One-person team where the PM is also the designer and engineer — the handoff is an internal note, not a cross-function artefact.
- Throwaway exploration not intended to inform downstream work.
