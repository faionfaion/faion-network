# Kill Criteria Template

## Summary
A one-page artifact filled in BEFORE a bet ships, naming the conditions under which the bet will be killed. The point is to remove the future founder's permission to negotiate with reality — kill criteria are pre-registered while incentives are clean. Outcome: every bet in the portfolio has a written, calendared kill date and threshold set, and the founder reviews them on a fixed cadence rather than ad hoc.

## Applies If
- You run a portfolio of 2+ small bets per year (indie hacker, micro-studio)
- You have noticed at least one prior project that lingered past usefulness
- The bet has a defined launch event (PH day, soft-launch cohort, first paid)
- You can name the metric that, if zero, makes the bet pointless

## Skip If
- The bet is your only product and pivoting is the alternative to killing
- The bet is a learning project with no revenue goal (research, OSS)
- Less than 14 days post-launch — too early to evaluate against thresholds
- You are inside a contractual obligation (paying customers, refund window)
