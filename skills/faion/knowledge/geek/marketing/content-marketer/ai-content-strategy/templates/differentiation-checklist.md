# Content Differentiation Checklist

## Generic Signals — REMOVE Before Publishing
- [ ] "According to [Industry Report]" without adding new interpretation
- [ ] "Best practices include..." without specific examples or data
- [ ] No statistics or original research
- [ ] No author experience or first-person perspective
- [ ] No contrarian viewpoint or opinion
- [ ] Advice that applies to everyone, not your specific audience

## Differentiation Signals — REQUIRE at Least 2
- [ ] Own research or proprietary data: _______________
- [ ] First-hand experience with specific outcome: _______________
- [ ] Expert quote or interview: _______________
- [ ] Specific case study with real numbers: _______________
- [ ] Contrarian take backed by evidence: _______________
- [ ] Real examples with measurable results: _______________
- [ ] Failed experiments and lessons learned: _______________
- [ ] Opinion on a contested topic in the industry: _______________

## E-E-A-T Signals — CHECK All Four
- [ ] Experience: "In my X years / When we tested / We found..."
- [ ] Expertise: Author credentials visible, methodology explained
- [ ] Authoritativeness: Citations from primary sources, recognized by peers
- [ ] Trustworthiness: Limitations stated, methodology transparent

## Verdict
- [ ] Publish as-is (2+ differentiation signals, all 4 E-E-A-T signals)
- [ ] Revise — add: _______________
- [ ] Reject — too generic, start over with different angle
