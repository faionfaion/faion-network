# Interviewer Training: STAR Method (2-Hour Module)

## Agenda

### Part 1: Why STAR? (20 min)

**Learning objective:** Understand why unstructured interviews fail and what STAR fixes.

- Problem: traditional interviews favor confident presenters over high performers
- Research anchor: McDaniel et al. (1994) — structured behavioral interviews are ~2x more predictive
- STAR as a predictive framework: past behavior predicts future behavior
- Demo: same question, unstructured vs. STAR-probed — compare information yield

**Discussion:** What questions have you been asked that felt unfair or uninformative?

---

### Part 2: The STAR Flow (30 min)

**Learning objective:** Know each component and its prescribed probe when missing.

| Component | Good signal | Missing signal → probe |
|-----------|------------|------------------------|
| Situation | Specific context, stakes, timeframe | "Tell me more about the context — when was this?" |
| Task | Clear personal responsibility | "What was YOUR specific role?" |
| Action | First-person, multiple concrete steps | "Walk me through exactly what YOU did." |
| Result | Quantified outcome or clear impact | "What was the end result? Can you quantify it?" |

**Common pitfalls:**
- Accepting "we" without probing for individual Action
- Accepting hypotheticals ("I would...")
- Forgetting to close on Result

---

### Part 3: Role-Play Practice (60 min)

**Round 1 (20 min):** Interviewer asks, candidate gives Scenario A (strong STAR)
- Score independently; compare; discuss variance

**Round 2 (20 min):** Interviewer asks, candidate gives Scenario B (missing Action)
- Practice probing for individual contribution
- Score independently; compare

**Round 3 (20 min):** Interviewer asks, candidate gives Scenario C (weak/red-flag)
- Practice staying curious without leading
- Score independently; debrief

---

### Part 4: Q&A and Calibration (10 min)

- Review the scorecard template
- Agree on behavioral anchor definitions for score 1, 3, 5 per competency
- Confirm: independent submission before debrief channel opens
