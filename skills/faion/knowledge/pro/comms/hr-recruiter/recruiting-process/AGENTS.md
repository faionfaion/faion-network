# Recruiting Process (Full-Cycle)

## Summary

A full-cycle talent acquisition framework covering ten methodologies from multi-channel sourcing through offer management. Each sub-methodology addresses a specific pipeline bottleneck: sourcing breadth, JD quality, candidate personas, passive outreach, ATS optimization, AI tooling, and metrics dashboards.

## Why

Full-cycle recruiting without a documented process devolves into reactive, channel-dependent hiring. Each gap in the pipeline (e.g., weak JD, over-reliance on job boards, no ATS automation) compounds: low application volume, poor screening signal, and slow offers pile up into long time-to-fill. Mapping each stage to a concrete sub-methodology makes bottlenecks addressable individually.

## When To Use

- Building a sourcing strategy for a new role type or market.
- Diagnosing why a specific stage is underperforming (see recruitment-funnel-optimization for diagnosis).
- Designing or auditing a JD, ATS workflow, or candidate outreach sequence.
- Implementing metrics reporting for a recruiting function.

## When NOT To Use

- Executive search where a retained firm is running the process — overlap creates confusion.
- Single-hire, one-time role where building pipelines and personas is overhead, not value.
- Compliance-audit contexts — use retention-compliance methodology instead.
