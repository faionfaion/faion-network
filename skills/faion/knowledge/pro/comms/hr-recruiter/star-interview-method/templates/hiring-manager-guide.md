# STAR Interview Guide for Hiring Managers

## Before the Interview

- [ ] Review the role's competency table (4-6 competencies + weights)
- [ ] Confirm your assigned competencies (for panel: each interviewer covers 1-2)
- [ ] Prepare 2-3 STAR questions per competency from the question bank
- [ ] Review candidate's resume — note any claims to probe in STAR format
- [ ] Block 15 min post-interview for scorecard completion (do it immediately)
- [ ] Send candidate the STAR prep brief 24 hours before

---

## During the Interview (55-minute format)

### Opening (5 min)
- Introduce yourself and your role
- Explain the format: "I'll ask about specific past experiences. I'll take notes."
- Set expectation: "I may ask follow-up questions to understand your specific contribution."

### STAR Questions (40 min)
For each question:
1. Ask the behavioral question (past-tense, open-ended)
2. Listen for Situation — if missing, probe: "Can you give me more context on when this was?"
3. Listen for Task — if missing: "What was your specific responsibility?"
4. Listen for Action — if "we" only: "What did YOU personally do?"
5. Listen for Result — always close: "What was the end result?" / "Can you quantify that?"
6. Take verbatim notes — do not interpret, just capture

**80/20 rule:** Candidate talks 80%, you talk 20%. If you're talking more, stop asking follow-ups.

### Candidate Questions (10 min)
- Answer honestly — never oversell
- If asked about compensation or offer: "That's handled by our recruiting team"

### Closing (5 min)
- Explain next steps and timeline
- Thank them — every candidate is a potential customer or referrer

---

## After the Interview

- [ ] Complete scorecard within 30 minutes (before memory fades)
- [ ] Submit scorecard before checking Slack, Teams, or email from other interviewers
- [ ] Do NOT discuss the candidate until all scorecards are submitted
- [ ] If you have a strong signal either direction: document the specific evidence, not the feeling

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why it hurts | Instead |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Asking "How would you..." | Hypothetical — no predictive value | "Tell me about a time when..." |
| Accepting "we did X" as Action | Re-attribution — can't score | "What did YOU specifically do?" |
| Forgetting to ask for Result | Incomplete STAR | Always close: "What was the outcome?" |
| Discussing before everyone submits | Anchoring bias | Wait for all scorecards |
| Scoring delivery, not content | Fluency bias | Focus on S/T/A/R completeness and evidence |
