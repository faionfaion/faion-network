# Retention and Hiring Compliance

## Summary

A paired framework covering two related HR domains: employee retention (stay interviews, exit analysis, 9-box talent reviews, internal mobility) and hiring compliance (legal requirements per funnel stage, DEI practices, process audits). Retention is cheapest before people quit; compliance failures are discovered during audits or litigation.

## Why

Exit interviews tell you why people left after the cost is sunk. Stay interviews with high performers surface flight risk early when action is still possible. On the compliance side, a single non-compliant question in an interview can trigger EEOC scrutiny; mapping legal requirements to each funnel stage turns compliance from a post-hoc audit into an in-process gate.

## When To Use

- Quarterly stay-interview cycle for high-performer retention.
- Annual or post-attrition talent review using the 9-box grid.
- Auditing a hiring process for legal compliance (protected-class question exposure, FCRA, pay equity).
- DEI initiative requiring structured sourcing, blind review, and diverse panels.
- Post-mortem after a compliance incident or exit pattern analysis.

## When NOT To Use

- Individual performance management — that belongs in the manager's 1:1 cadence, not a retention framework.
- Executive compensation strategy — requires specialized total-rewards expertise.
- Jurisdictions with highly specific labor law (medical, aviation, finance) — consult legal counsel first; this framework is not legal advice.
