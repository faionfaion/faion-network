# Recruitment Funnel Optimization

## Summary

A stage-by-stage framework for measuring and improving candidate conversion across six hiring phases: Awareness, Application, Screening, Interview, Offer, and Onboarding. Each stage has a key metric, industry benchmark, and concrete optimization levers. Apply it when time-to-fill or offer-acceptance rate deviates from benchmarks.

## Why

High candidate drop-off at any single stage inflates time-to-fill and cost-per-hire without a visible root cause. Funnel visibility converts a vague "we're struggling to hire" complaint into a specific, addressable bottleneck — e.g., offer-acceptance at 70% (below 85% benchmark) triggered by compensation gap, solvable with market benchmarking.

## When To Use

- Time-to-fill exceeds 45 days for a role type that should close faster.
- Offer acceptance rate drops below 85%.
- No current tracking of conversion rates between funnel stages.
- Preparing a quarterly or monthly recruiting metrics report.
- Post-mortem on a failed hire or stalled pipeline.

## When NOT To Use

- Single isolated open role with no historical data — benchmark comparison is meaningless.
- Roles with structurally different funnels (executive search, agency-sourced, internal transfer) — apply role-segmented benchmarks instead.
- Optimizing volume only without tracking quality-of-hire — risks flooding the funnel with mismatched candidates.
