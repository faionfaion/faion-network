# STAR Interviewer Guide

A concise reference for hiring managers and interviewers using the STAR behavioral method.

---

## Before the Interview

- [ ] Review the role's key competencies and their behavioral anchors.
- [ ] Prepare 4-6 STAR behavioral questions mapped to the competencies you are assessing.
- [ ] Review the candidate's resume — note specific experiences to probe.
- [ ] Block your calendar; no interruptions during the interview.
- [ ] Open a blank scorecard to take notes in real time.

---

## Interview Structure

### Opening (5 min)
- Introduce yourself and your role.
- Explain the format: "I'll be asking behavioral questions — I'm interested in specific situations from your past experience."
- Set expectations: "I may ask follow-up questions to get more detail. That's normal, not a red flag."

### STAR Questions (40 min)

For each question:

1. **Ask** the behavioral question. Wait for the full answer before probing.
2. **Listen** for all four STAR components as the candidate speaks.
3. **Probe** for missing components:
   - Missing Situation: "Can you give me more context about when this happened?"
   - Missing Task: "What was your specific responsibility in that situation?"
   - Missing Action: "Walk me through exactly what YOU did — what steps did you take?"
   - Missing Result: "What was the outcome? Do you have any numbers to share?"
4. **Note** specific actions and results, not impressions or adjectives.

### Candidate Questions (10 min)
- Allow the candidate to ask questions.
- Answer honestly; do not oversell.

### Closing (5 min)
- Explain next steps and timeline.
- Thank the candidate.

---

## After the Interview

- [ ] Complete the scorecard immediately after the interview — not hours later.
- [ ] Submit the scorecard before the debrief meeting.
- [ ] Record specific examples and quotes, not general impressions.
- [ ] Do not discuss your assessment with other interviewers until the debrief.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Accepting "we" answers without probing | Cannot score personal contribution |
| Letting a compelling story skip the Result probe | Outcome is the strongest scoring signal |
| Sharing your score before debrief | Anchors other interviewers to your view |
| Taking notes on impressions ("seemed nervous") | Not scorable evidence |
| Asking hypothetical instead of behavioral | "What would you do" predicts less than "what did you do" |
