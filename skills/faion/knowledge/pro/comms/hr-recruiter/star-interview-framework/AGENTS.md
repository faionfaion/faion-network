# STAR Interview Framework

## Summary

A structured behavioral interviewing technique where every question elicits a four-part response: Situation (context), Task (responsibility), Action (candidate's specific steps), Result (measurable outcome). Past behavior under real conditions is the strongest available predictor of future job performance; STAR enforces completeness and comparability across candidates.

## Why

Without a response framework, candidates give generic, hypothetical, or team-credit answers that interviewers cannot score or compare. STAR follow-up probes ("What was YOUR role, not the team's?", "What were the specific numbers?") force specificity. Scored against a rubric, STAR responses become defensible hiring evidence rather than gut impression.

## When To Use

- Behavioral rounds in any structured interview process.
- Evaluating leadership, communication, collaboration, adaptability, or problem-solving competencies.
- Building a question bank for a new role or competency model.
- Training interviewers who ask vague or hypothetical questions.

## When NOT To Use

- Technical skills assessment (live coding, system design) — STAR is not the right structure.
- Screening for factual qualifications (certifications, tool experience) — a direct question is faster.
- When the candidate has very limited work history (new grads) — combine with situational ("What would you do if...") questions.
