# STAR Interview Framework

## Summary

**One-sentence:** The STAR framework (Situation-Task-Action-Result) is a structured behavioral interviewing technique that elicits four-part responses from candidates, forcing specificity and comparability across candidates.

**One-paragraph:** The STAR framework (Situation-Task-Action-Result) is a structured behavioral interviewing technique that elicits four-part responses from candidates, forcing specificity and comparability across candidates. Without a response framework, candidates give generic, hypothetical, or team-credit answers that interviewers cannot score or compare. STAR follow-up probes force specificity; scored against a rubric, STAR responses become defensible hiring evidence.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Behavioral rounds in any structured interview process.
- Evaluating leadership, communication, collaboration, adaptability, or problem-solving competencies.
- Building a question bank for a new role or competency model.
- Training interviewers who ask vague or hypothetical questions.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- Technical skills assessment (live coding, system design) — STAR is not the right structure.
- Screening for factual qualifications (certifications, tool experience) — a direct question is faster.
- When the candidate has very limited work history (new grads) — combine with situational ("What would you do if...") questions.
