# Stakeholder Engagement (Advanced)

## Summary

A structured process for moving stakeholders from their current engagement level (Unaware, Resistant, Neutral, Supportive, Leading) to a desired level through targeted, evidence-based activities. Each engagement level change requires a quoted behavioural signal — not a vibe — as evidence. The core tool is the Engagement Assessment Matrix: per-stakeholder current level, desired level, gap, and a specific activity plan with cadence.

## Why

Passive stakeholder lists decay. Resistant stakeholders do not disappear — they become active blockers. Supportive stakeholders lose enthusiasm without maintenance. The five-level model (Unaware → Leading) gives a concrete, auditable measure of relationship health that replaces gut-feel with observable signals (meeting attendance, response time, public statements).

## When To Use

- Stakeholder Register exists but the project lacks champions, has dormant supporters, or active resisters
- Pre-launch and change-management work where adoption depends on department heads
- Long projects (over 6 months) where engagement levels drift — quarterly re-assessment needed
- Coalition-building for portfolio shifts (M&A, reorg, platform migration)
- Public-facing launches where end users are Unaware and need to reach Supportive

## When NOT To Use

- One-off small tasks with no organisational politics — overhead exceeds value
- Adversarial negotiations (procurement, legal disputes) — engagement implies collaboration; use negotiation playbooks instead
- Crisis communications — different discipline, different cadence and tone
- Projects under 4 weeks with a single decision-maker — a one-page RACI is sufficient
