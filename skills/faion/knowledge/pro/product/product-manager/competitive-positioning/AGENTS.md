# Competitive Positioning (PM)

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Competitive positioning defines how your product is uniquely valuable compared to alternatives.

**One-paragraph:** Competitive positioning defines how your product is uniquely valuable compared to alternatives. The PM uses April Dunford's six-component model: competitive alternatives, unique attributes, value translation, best-fit customer, market category, and positioning statement. Every roadmap initiative must pass a positioning-fit gate (score 0-3) before entering a sprint; positioning is a decision filter, not a deliverable.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Positioning statement was last touched more than 2 quarters ago and roadmap is drifting toward feature parity.
- New PM onboarding to an existing product — internalise positioning before approving any backlog item.
- Roadmap planning quarter — positioning is the gate every initiative passes through.
- Pricing change or new tier launch — positioning constrains which features go in which tier.
- Feature kill decisions — use positioning canvas as evidence to deprecate features that reinforce nothing.
- Cross-functional alignment audit — marketing, sales, support, and product imply different positioning.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- Marketing copy iteration — that's the CMO/marketing manager's domain; PM contributes inputs only.
- Brand/visual identity refresh — positioning is strategic, brand is expressive.
- Pre-PMF with fewer than 50 customers — run customer discovery first.
- Single-PM startups where PM = founder = marketer = sales — use product-planning variant directly.
- Highly regulated B2B where positioning is dictated by procurement RFPs and analyst categories.
