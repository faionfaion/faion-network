## Pricing Research Questions

### Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter (4 questions, ask in this order)
1. "At what price would [product] be so expensive you would not consider buying it?"
2. "At what price would [product] be expensive but still worth considering?"
3. "At what price would [product] be a good deal for what it offers?"
4. "At what price would [product] be so cheap you would question its quality?"

### Willingness-to-Pay (avoid hypotheticals — use these instead)
- "How much do you currently pay monthly for tools that solve [problem]?"
- "What would make a $49/month plan clearly worth it to you?" (open-ended)

## Satisfaction Research Questions

### Overall satisfaction (5-point)
"How satisfied are you with [product] overall?"
(1 = Very dissatisfied, 5 = Very satisfied)

### NPS (0-10)
"How likely are you to recommend [product] to a colleague or friend?"
(0 = Not at all likely, 10 = Extremely likely)

## Feature Prioritization Questions

### Ranking (max 7 items)
"Rank these features by importance to you (1 = most important, [N] = least important)."

### MaxDiff (best for 8+ items)
"Of these 4 features, which is MOST important to you? Which is LEAST important?"
[Repeat across 3+ sets covering all features at least twice]

### Importance vs. Satisfaction matrix
For each feature: "How important is this feature to you? (1-5)" and "How satisfied are you with it? (1-5)"
→ Quadrant: high importance + low satisfaction = top build priority
