# Distribution Channel Research

## Summary

Distribution channel research identifies and evaluates the pathways through which customers discover and adopt a product at the market level: traffic-source mix across incumbents, partner/reseller/marketplace landscape, and benchmark channel economics (CPC/CPM/CAC ranges with confidence bands). It answers "how do people in this market actually find and buy products like this?" before committing GTM spend.

## Why

"If you build it, they will come" fails because founders pick channels based on preference rather than where the buyer segment actually lives. Market-level channel-share data (not guesswork) reveals which channels move volume, what the margin stack-up looks like through each path, and which partner types control access to the buyer — information that prevents wasted spend on wrong channels.

## When To Use

- Market entry: need to know which channels actually move volume in a target market before committing GTM budget.
- Investor/strategy decks requiring sourced channel-economics slide with market-level CPC/CAC ranges.
- Channel-share studies: quantifying what % of category revenue flows through SEO vs paid vs partner vs direct.
- Partner-landscape mapping for B2B: identifying distributors, resellers, MSPs, and integration partners.
- Refreshing channel benchmarks when numbers in the deck are older than 12 months.
- Pricing/business-model research that depends on channel margin stack-ups.

## When NOT To Use

- Founder needs creative/copy/test-design help — route to growth-marketer.
- Pre-PMF single-product validation where the question is "does anyone want this" — premature optimization.
- Pure brand campaigns with no recurring channel decision.
- Markets where channel data is structurally unavailable (private niches, NDAs, regulated verticals) — output will be conjecture; stop and flag.
- When the requester has not specified a market boundary (geo + segment + buyer) — denominator is undefined.
