# Distribution Channel Research

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Distribution channel research identifies and evaluates the pathways through which customers discover and adopt a product at the market level: traffic-source mix across incumbents, partner/reseller/marketplace landscape, and benchmark channel economics (CPC/CPM/CAC ranges with confidence bands).

**One-paragraph:** Distribution channel research identifies and evaluates the pathways through which customers discover and adopt a product at the market level: traffic-source mix across incumbents, partner/reseller/marketplace landscape, and benchmark channel economics (CPC/CPM/CAC ranges with confidence bands). It answers "how do people in this market actually find and buy products like this?" before committing GTM spend.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Market entry: need to know which channels actually move volume in a target market before committing GTM budget.
- Investor/strategy decks requiring sourced channel-economics slide with market-level CPC/CAC ranges.
- Channel-share studies: quantifying what % of category revenue flows through SEO vs paid vs partner vs direct.
- Partner-landscape mapping for B2B: identifying distributors, resellers, MSPs, and integration partners.
- Refreshing channel benchmarks when numbers in the deck are older than 12 months.
- Pricing/business-model research that depends on channel margin stack-ups.
- Market entry analysis: a client/founder needs to know which channels actually move volume in a target market before committing GTM spend (e.g. "is dental SaaS sold via direct, marketplace, or channel partners in DACH?").
- Investor / strategy decks where the channel-economics slide must show market-level CPC/CAC ranges and partner take-rates with sourced citations, not back-of-envelope guesses.
- Channel-share studies: quantifying what % of category revenue flows through SEO vs paid vs partner vs direct sales across the top 10 incumbents.
- Partner-landscape mapping for B2B: identifying every distributor, reseller, MSP, marketplace, and integration partner that touches the buyer journey, with reach/exclusivity/take-rate per partner.
- Pricing & business-model research that depends on channel margin stack-ups (e.g. SaaS direct 70% gross margin vs. AWS Marketplace listing −3% take vs. reseller −20%).
- Refreshing channel benchmarks when incumbent CPC/CPM/CAC numbers in the deck are >12 months old.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- Founder needs creative/copy/test-design help — route to growth-marketer.
- Pre-PMF single-product validation where the question is "does anyone want this" — premature optimization.
- Pure brand campaigns with no recurring channel decision.
- Markets where channel data is structurally unavailable (private niches, NDAs, regulated verticals) — output will be conjecture; stop and flag.
- When the requester has not specified a market boundary (geo + segment + buyer) — denominator is undefined.
- Founder needs creative/copy/test-design help — that is a growth-marketing job, not market research; route to pro/marketing/growth-marketer or the researcher-variant of this methodology.
- Pre-PMF single-product validation where the question is "does anyone want this" — channel research at that stage is premature optimization.
- Pure brand campaigns or one-off launches with no recurring channel decision.
- Markets where channel data is structurally unavailable (private niches, enterprise with NDAs, regulated verticals with no ad libraries) — output will be conjecture; flag and stop.
- When the requester has not specified a market boundary (geo + segment + buyer); without a boundary the channel-share denominator is undefined.
