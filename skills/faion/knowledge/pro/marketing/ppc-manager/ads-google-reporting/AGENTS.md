# Google Ads Reporting & Optimization

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Google Ads data is abundant but insights are rare.

**One-paragraph:** Google Ads data is abundant but insights are rare. Systematic reporting, segment analysis, metric diagnosis, and optimization cycles transform raw data into actionable improvements. This methodology teaches you how to read reports, diagnose issues, and optimize based on data rather than gut feeling.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Running continuous paid search campaigns and need a systematic weekly/monthly optimization process.
- Diagnosing performance drops: CPA increasing, impression share falling, or conversion rate declining.
- Building institutional knowledge: documenting what works in your market through repeated testing cycles.
- Multi-campaign management: prioritizing which campaigns and keywords deserve optimization effort first.
- Scaling spend: understanding which levers (bids, budgets, targeting) move the metrics that matter.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- New accounts with under 30 days history—algorithmic variance dominates treatment effect; wait for more data.
- Daily fluctuations—small day-to-day noise is normal; focus on weekly and monthly trends.
- Campaigns with fewer than 100 conversions per variant—insufficient data for diagnosis; ship the better option and move on.
- Holiday peaks (Black Friday, Q4)—exogenous demand swamps creative and targeting changes; use proven creatives instead.
