# Meta Creative Best Practices

## Summary

AIDA-driven creative production for Meta Ads: stop the scroll with a strong visual hook (faces, before/after, bold contrast), write benefit-led copy with a tested hook formula (question/claim/curiosity/social-proof/direct), produce both 1:1 and 9:16 format variants, and test creatives systematically by isolating one variable at a time. Refresh every 2-4 weeks when CTR declines or frequency exceeds 3.

## Why

Targeting configuration accounts for roughly 20-30% of ad performance; creative accounts for the rest. Most advertisers spend 90% of their time on targeting and wonder why ads underperform. A single creative change — hook wording or visual type — can shift CTR from 0.8% to 1.5%+. Without a testing framework, every campaign iteration is a guess.

## When To Use

- Briefing or producing new Meta ad creatives (image, video, carousel).
- Setting up a creative testing structure with multiple hook/visual variants.
- Diagnosing low CTR or high CPA when audience and budget are not the constraint.
- Planning a creative refresh after fatigue signals appear.

## When NOT To Use

- When budget is under $20-50 per creative — not enough signal to judge performance.
- When the offer or value proposition is still unclear — creative cannot rescue a confusing product.
- For Google Display or Search creative — specs and conventions differ significantly.
