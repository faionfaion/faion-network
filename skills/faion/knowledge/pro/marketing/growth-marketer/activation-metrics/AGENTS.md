# Activation Metrics

## Summary

A methodology for defining, measuring, and improving the activation rate — the percentage of signups who reach their first value moment (the "Aha moment"). Covers how to identify the right activation event by correlating candidate actions with D30 retention, how to measure time-bounded activation rates, benchmark ranges by product type, and a worked optimization funnel template.

## Why

Improving activation is often the highest-ROI growth lever because the same acquisition spend produces 3-5x more converted customers. The activation event must correlate with retention, not just signal setup completion — wrong activation targets (e.g., "created profile" instead of "completed first task") optimize for vanity actions that don't predict revenue.

## When To Use

- Users sign up but don't return after the first session.
- Onboarding completion is high but D30 retention is low — possible activation misdefinition.
- Pre-scaling acquisition: fix activation before spending more on top-of-funnel.
- Redesigning onboarding flow and need a metric to validate the change.

## When NOT To Use

- Pre-PMF: if core value is undefined, activation measurement is premature.
- Enterprise SaaS with multi-month sales cycles — activation happens over weeks, not sessions.
- Products with no analytics infrastructure — defining the metric without tracking it produces no signal.
