# Agency Niche Positioning

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Forces a micro-agency to pick a vertical narrower than feels safe, refuse out-of-niche work, and repackage past portfolio to look like the target niche.

**One-paragraph:** growth-brand-positioning is product-flavored. Agencies need vertical positioning that competes on category authority, not features. Mechanism: pick one vertical at the intersection of (a) ≥3 closed projects, (b) ICP buyer willing to pay top-decile rates, (c) underserved by 'full-service' agencies. Output: a written niche statement, an 'out-of-niche' refusal rubric, and a portfolio rewrite plan.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- agency size 1-8 people, founder-led
- current positioning is horizontal or 'full-service'
- ≥3 closed projects in at least one identifiable vertical
- founder has authority to refuse work

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- agency >20 people with multiple verticals already siloed — use portfolio strategy instead
- freelancer with no team — use pro/marketing/niche-positioning-for-solo-dev
- agency operating in a single client account — captive, not niche-able

## Prerequisites

- list of last 24 months of closed projects with revenue and vertical tag
- list of last 12 months of refused or unfit leads
- founder consent to lose 20-40% of current pipeline during transition
