# Contractor Basics for Solopreneurs

## Summary

**One-sentence:** A five-step framework for solopreneurs hiring their first contractors: Identify (delegation matrix and ROI calc), Find (sourcing channels and job-post template), Evaluate (screening stages, red/green flags), Onboard (paid test task before commitment), Manage (communication norms and tool setup).

**One-paragraph:** A five-step framework for solopreneurs hiring their first contractors: Identify (delegation matrix and ROI calc), Find (sourcing channels and job-post template), Evaluate (screening stages, red/green flags), Onboard (paid test task before commitment), Manage (communication norms and tool setup). The core rule: always run a paid test task on a real deliverable before any ongoing engagement; never sign a contract or make a hire autonomously — human approval required at every commitment point.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Solo founder ready to delegate first paid work
- Drafting a job post, paid-test-task brief, or applicant scoring rubric
- Computing ROI of delegation vs DIY before committing budget
- Pre-flight screening: flagging red/green signals in a candidate dossier
- Re-evaluating which tasks are core vs delegate-worthy after a workload spike

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- Hiring W-2 employees — misclassification risk; use hr-recruiter knowledge
- Managing an existing contractor relationship — use ops-contractor-management
- Procurement of SaaS vendors or agencies on retainer — different structure (MSA + SOW)
- Hiring in regulated roles (legal, medical, financial advice) — needs licensed-professional checks
- Markets where contractor classification is legally constrained (CA AB5, EU dependent-contractor tests) — escalate to legal counsel first
