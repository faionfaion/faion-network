# Partnership Strategy

## Summary

A five-stage framework for creating mutually beneficial partnerships that extend reach beyond your own audience: identify partnership type (affiliate, co-marketing, integration, reseller, white-label), score candidates on reach/relevance/trust/effort/risk, structure the deal with clear mutual value, outreach with the partner's benefit in the first paragraph, and maintain with a 30-60-90 review cadence.

## Why

Organic and paid channels saturate; partnerships compound — the same co-marketing webinar can be repeated quarterly at near-zero marginal cost. One-sided proposals (80% of cold outreach) get binned; partnerships that start with a small concrete pilot ("single webinar in two weeks") succeed because they're low-commitment tests of fit before multi-quarter commitments.

## When To Use

- Distribution-constrained: product is solid and you need to reach a defined audience that gathers around another product
- Building integrations that reduce churn (becoming a Slack/Notion add-on)
- Co-marketing webinars, bundles, or guides with a complementary tool sharing your ICP
- Reseller or white-label expansion without a dedicated sales team

## When NOT To Use

- Pre-PMF: partnerships add coordination overhead; spend that energy on direct customer interviews instead
- Unclear value-prop: partners cannot promote what they don't understand — fail the one-pager test first
- High-burn 3-month runway: partnerships compound over 6–18 months, not 90 days
- When churn is the real problem: partnerships add top-of-funnel but won't fix retention
