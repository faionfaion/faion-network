# Press & PR Coverage

## Summary

Relationship-driven press outreach: identify journalists by tier (Tier 3 niche first, Tier 1 only after coverage history), research each journalist's beat and recent articles, craft a newsworthy story angle (first/trend/data/milestone/contrarian), send a personalized pitch under 150 words with one specific article reference and three concrete assets offered, then maximize coverage impact through social sharing and relationship maintenance.

## Why

Generic press releases sent to Tier 1 outlets without prior coverage history are ignored — journalists need stories that serve their readers, not product announcements. Starting with Tier 3 builds a coverage record and improves Tier 1 pitch credibility. Each pitch must cite a specific, non-obvious insight from the journalist's recent work; flattery-only openers ("Loved your piece") are screened instantly.

## When To Use

- Announcing a product launch, funding round, major milestone, or original research.
- Building a journalist database and coverage pipeline before a major launch.
- Daily HARO/Qwoted triage: filtering relevant queries and drafting pitches for human review.
- Maintaining and updating the press kit (founder bio, boilerplate, assets).

## When NOT To Use

- Pre-product or pre-traction — without a real story (data, milestone, launch) no outreach volume earns coverage.
- Embargoed announcements or exclusives — relationship work that requires human-only contact.
- Crisis communications or legal-sensitive PR — agent-drafted statements carry legal risk.
- Tier-1 auto-pitching — TechCrunch, NYT, WSJ reporters will blacklist senders on first templated email.
