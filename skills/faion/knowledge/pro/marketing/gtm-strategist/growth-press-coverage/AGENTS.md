# Press & PR Coverage

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Relationship-driven press outreach: identify journalists by tier (Tier 3 niche first, Tier 1 only after coverage history), research each journalist's beat and recent articles, craft a newsworthy story angle (first/trend/data/milestone/contrarian), send a personalized pitch under 150 words with one specific article reference and three concrete assets offered, then maximize coverage impact through social sharing and relationship maintenance.

**One-paragraph:** Relationship-driven press outreach: identify journalists by tier (Tier 3 niche first, Tier 1 only after coverage history), research each journalist's beat and recent articles, craft a newsworthy story angle (first/trend/data/milestone/contrarian), send a personalized pitch under 150 words with one specific article reference and three concrete assets offered, then maximize coverage impact through social sharing and relationship maintenance.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Announcing a product launch, funding round, major milestone, or original research.
- Building a journalist database and coverage pipeline before a major launch.
- Daily HARO/Qwoted triage: filtering relevant queries and drafting pitches for human review.
- Maintaining and updating the press kit (founder bio, boilerplate, assets).

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- Pre-product or pre-traction — without a real story (data, milestone, launch) no outreach volume earns coverage.
- Embargoed announcements or exclusives — relationship work that requires human-only contact.
- Crisis communications or legal-sensitive PR — agent-drafted statements carry legal risk.
- Tier-1 auto-pitching — TechCrunch, NYT, WSJ reporters will blacklist senders on first templated email.
