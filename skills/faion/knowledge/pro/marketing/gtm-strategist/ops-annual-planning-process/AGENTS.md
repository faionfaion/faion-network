# Annual Planning Process

## Summary

A structured five-stage planning framework for solopreneurs: Reflect (year-in-review with cited metrics), Envision (12-month vision statement), Strategize (max 3 priorities — hard cap enforced), Plan (annual → quarterly → monthly task tree), Execute (weekly/monthly/quarterly review rhythm). The non-negotiable rule: cap annual priorities at 3 and enforce a quantified kill-switch and leading indicator for each key result.

## Why

Founders who plan day-to-day without a yearly direction accumulate effort without compounding. Annual planning forces a "stop doing" list, exposes misallocated time, and creates quarterly checkpoints that surface drift before it becomes permanent. LLM-generated plans without forced prioritization converge on 7-item lists where nothing is real. The ≤3 priority constraint is the mechanism that makes the plan actionable.

## When To Use

- December/January: annual review + goal setting
- End of each quarter: quarterly review + next-quarter plan
- First week of each month: monthly check-in against quarterly goals
- When metrics show meaningful drift from targets (>15% off expected)
- Decomposing an annual goal into a monthly/weekly task tree

## When NOT To Use

- Setting the founder's vision — agents produce regression-to-mean prose; founders write vision, agents critique and operationalize
- Strategic pivots based on new market signal — qualitative, narrative, requires founder ownership
- Personal goals (energy, satisfaction, burnout) — agents have no telemetry on these
- Salary, equity, or capital-allocation decisions inside the plan — financial-planning domain
- Org-wide planning at more than 20 people — use PM-agile or PM-traditional methodologies instead
