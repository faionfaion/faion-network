# Influencer Marketing

## Summary

A four-phase FIND-FIT-FLOW-MEASURE framework for reaching new audiences through creator partnerships: discover micro-influencers (10K–100K) by engagement quality rather than follower count, vet for audience match and red flags (bought followers, bot comments), outreach with a personalized brief, execute with FTC-compliant disclosures, and track ROI via UTM codes and unique discount codes per creator.

## Why

Most influencer campaigns fail because brands select by follower count, produce inauthentic briefs, and skip attribution setup. Micro-influencers at 10K–100K deliver the best ROI for niche products: high trust, lower cost, and real engagement. Engagement-rate gating (above platform averages) eliminates inflated accounts before budget is spent.

## When To Use

- Reaching new audiences when SEO and paid ads have plateaued or have unfavorable unit economics
- Launching a product to a niche where 10K–100K creators drive purchase decisions (DTC, SaaS prosumer, creator tools)
- Building a UGC pipeline cheaply via gifting + reuse rights instead of in-house production
- Pre-launch seeding: placing product with micro creators in target niche to generate D-day content

## When NOT To Use

- B2B enterprise (ACV above $10K) where buying committees ignore creator endorsements
- Highly regulated verticals (medical, financial advice, securities) — FTC + sector compliance rules are brittle
- Marketing budgets under $5K — fixed costs of vetting, outreach, and contracts erode ROI; start with referral programs instead
- Products with no demonstrable 30-second hero moment — creators cannot make compelling content
