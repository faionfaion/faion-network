# Ops Legal Basics

## Summary

Establishes the minimum viable legal foundation for a new online business: entity formation, essential policies (ToS, Privacy Policy, Cookie, Refund), key privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, CAN-SPAM), IP protection priorities, and contract essentials. Treats legal work as infrastructure — set it up once correctly before taking real money, not after.

## Why

Skipping entity formation means liability flows personally; skipping policies exposes you to fines (GDPR up to 4% of annual turnover) and user claims; unprotected IP is recoverable only with legal cost. Most founders pay for legal negligence retroactively — this methodology front-loads the minimum set that covers 80% of early-stage risk at template cost.

## When To Use

- Pre-launch: choosing business entity before first revenue
- Setting up ToS, Privacy Policy, Cookie Policy for a new web product
- Assessing privacy-regulation exposure (GDPR/CCPA/CAN-SPAM) for a specific user geography
- Drafting first-version contracts: customer agreement, contractor NDA, affiliate agreement
- Trademark scoping before naming or launch (USPTO pre-search)
- Budget triage: templates vs. lawyer for each legal need

## When NOT To Use

- Litigation, demand letters, IP enforcement — adversarial work requires lawyer-led effort
- Investment/fundraising/M&A (term sheets, SAFEs, share purchase) — too high stakes for templates
- Regulated verticals: HIPAA, FINRA, EU AI Act, FDA — domain-specific counsel is mandatory
- Cross-border data transfers requiring SCCs/DPF — needs counsel and DPA inventory
- Employment law (W-2 hires, terminations) — out of scope for solopreneur basics
