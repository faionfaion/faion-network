# Legal Fundamentals for Founders: ToS, Privacy, and Trademark Basics

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Before accepting customers, you need three legal documents: Terms of Service that govern the relationship and limit your liability, a Privacy Policy that discloses how you collect and handle user data (required by GDPR, CCPA), and trademark registration to protect your brand name.

**One-paragraph:** Before accepting customers, you need three legal documents: Terms of Service that govern the relationship and limit your liability, a Privacy Policy that discloses how you collect and handle user data (required by GDPR, CCPA), and trademark registration to protect your brand name. A ToS must cover payment, billing cycles, intellectual property ownership, disclaimers, liability caps, termination, and governing law. A Privacy Policy must detail data collection, use, retention, user rights, and security. Trademark registration via the US Patent and Trademark Office protects your brand.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- Before launching a SaaS product or accepting paying customers.
- Before collecting any user data (emails, usage logs, payment info).
- Before using your brand name publicly (product launch, press coverage, social media).
- When expanding into new markets or geographies (GDPR, CCPA, and local regulations vary).
- After receiving legal notice or a cease-and-desist from a competitor.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- Free projects with no user data collection and no business intent (personal blog, hobby projects).
- Internal tools used only by your company and employees (no external users, no external data).
- Services exclusively for a single client under a custom contract (bespoke legal agreement replaces standard ToS).
