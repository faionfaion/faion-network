# Community-Led Growth (CLG)

## Summary

A growth strategy where a community of engaged users drives acquisition, retention, and expansion instead of company marketing. Core principle: seed before launch (30+ hand-invited founding members), deliver one concrete value drop per week (AMA, template, behind-the-scenes), track DAU/MAU and peer-help response rate (not member count), and cap public community size around 1K before adding a qualification step to maintain the "2.3x engagement" micro-community premium.

## Why

Rising CAC, ad fatigue, and algorithm changes erode paid acquisition. Communities create an owned-audience moat: 24% higher purchase value, 46% higher CLV, 32% CAC reduction, $6.40 ROI per $1 invested (CMX/McKinsey aggregate benchmarks — individual results require 2-6 month payback horizon). Discord's 73% DAU/MAU ratio makes it the default 2026 platform for real-time community; Slack fits B2B/professional contexts.

## When To Use

- Acquisition channels (paid ads, SEO) are degrading and CAC is rising.
- Product has natural sharing, template, or peer-help surface (design tools, no-code builders, dev tools, learning products).
- You already have 50-500 power users to convert into a seeded community before opening publicly.
- Building a micro-community (&lt;= 2K members, high engagement) rather than a large vanity Discord.

## When NOT To Use

- Pre-PMF products with fewer than 50 active users — there is no community to seed.
- Highly transactional products with no conversation surface (utility tools, one-shot purchases).
- Compliance-sensitive verticals (medical, financial advice) where moderation liability exceeds acquisition value.
- Solo-founder bandwidth-locked situations where consistent moderation is impossible.
