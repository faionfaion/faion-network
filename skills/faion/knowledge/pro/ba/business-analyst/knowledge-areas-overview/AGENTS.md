# BA Knowledge Areas Overview

## Summary

The Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK) organizes BA work into six knowledge areas: BA Planning and Monitoring, Elicitation and Collaboration, Requirements Lifecycle Management, Strategy Analysis, Requirements Analysis and Design Definition, and Solution Evaluation. This overview is a routing index, not an executable deliverable — it answers which knowledge areas apply to a given situation and directs the agent to the specific sub-methodology to load next.

## Why

Without a framework map, BA activities appear as a random collection of tasks. Teams skip critical areas, produce inconsistent deliverables, and cannot explain which activities are appropriate for their project type. The BABOK knowledge area model provides a coherent structure that applies across waterfall, agile, and hybrid delivery contexts, while the five perspectives (Agile, BI, IT, Business Architecture, BPM) adapt it to specific domains.

## When To Use

- Project kickoff where the BA has no plan and needs to select which knowledge areas apply.
- Scoping a BA effort for a new feature or epic.
- Auditing an in-flight project for BA knowledge area coverage gaps.
- Onboarding a new BA or AI agent to a domain — load this first to get the routing map.
- Routing inside a multi-agent BA pipeline to decide which sub-agent runs next.

## When NOT To Use

- You already know the specific technique needed — load the target methodology directly, skip the overview.
- Small single-feature task with one stakeholder and known scope — overhead exceeds value.
- Pure software design with no business requirements — use software-architect skills instead.
- Agile teams with a working backlog and clear DoR/DoD — BABOK formalism adds friction.
- Certification study — this file is operational context, not exam preparation.
