# Design System Impact Queue

## Summary

**One-sentence:** Standing queue that captures design-system implications surfaced during reviews / drifts / RFCs and triages them on a fixed cadence so they never bleed into ad-hoc component churn.

**One-paragraph:** Standing queue that captures design-system implications surfaced during reviews / drifts / RFCs and triages them on a fixed cadence so they never bleed into ad-hoc component churn. The methodology pins inputs to citable sources, runs ≥5 testable rules to reject fabricated or un-anchored outputs, and emits an artefact that a downstream agent or named human reviewer can sign off without re-deriving the reasoning. Decision tree in `content/06-decision-tree.xml` routes the caller to apply-or-skip based on observable signals.

**Ефективно для:**

- Catching 'this should be a token, not a one-off' moments during product reviews.
- Routing impact items to the right pillar owner without a side-channel chat.
- Closing the loop on which queued items shipped vs got rejected.
- Producing the weekly design-ops digest from a single source.
- Surfacing patterns that justify a refactor or new component.

## Applies If (ALL must hold)

- The triggering activity for design-system-impact-queue appears in the operator's workload at least once per cycle.
- The operator has authority to act on the artefact this methodology produces (write access, sign-off rights).
- A named consumer exists for the output — either a human reviewer or a downstream agent.
- An auditable source-of-truth is available for the inputs this methodology requires.

## Skip If (ANY kills it)

- One-off, never-to-repeat work — methodology overhead does not pay back.
- No named consumer for the artefact — output will be orphaned regardless of quality.
- Inputs are not available from a citable source-of-truth (paraphrased substitutes are worse than skipping).
