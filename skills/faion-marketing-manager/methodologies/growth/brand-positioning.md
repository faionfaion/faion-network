## Problem

Your product works, but you blend in with competitors. When prospects evaluate options, you're "another one of those" instead of "the one for me." Without clear positioning, you compete on price and features - a race to the bottom.

Positioning is about owning a space in your customer's mind. It's the most important strategic decision you'll make.

---

## Framework

Strong positioning answers one question clearly:

```
What makes you the obvious choice for a specific customer?
```

**The positioning formula:**
```
For [TARGET CUSTOMER]
Who [SITUATION/NEED]
[PRODUCT] is the [CATEGORY]
That [KEY BENEFIT]
Unlike [ALTERNATIVES]
We [UNIQUE DIFFERENTIATOR]
```

### Step 1: Define Your Target Customer

**Be ruthlessly specific:**

| Too Broad | Specific |
|-----------|----------|
| Businesses | B2B SaaS companies 10-50 employees |
| Developers | JavaScript developers building web apps |
| Entrepreneurs | Solo founders with technical background |

**Target customer template:**
```
Our target customer is a [role]
at a [company type]
who is trying to [goal]
but struggles with [obstacle].
```

**Example:**
```
Our target customer is a marketing manager
at a Series A SaaS startup
who is trying to increase trial-to-paid conversion
but struggles with limited engineering resources.
```

### Step 2: Understand Your Competitive Landscape

**Map your alternatives:**

| Alternative Type | Example |
|------------------|---------|
| Direct competitors | Similar products |
| Indirect competitors | Different solutions to same problem |
| Status quo | Doing nothing, manual process |
| Substitutes | DIY, hire someone |

**Competitive positioning map:**

Create a 2x2 grid with your key differentiating attributes:

```
                High Price
                    |
        Enterprise  |  Premium
        Solutions   |  Products
                    |
Low ----------------+---------------- High
Complexity          |              Simplicity
                    |
        Free/OSS    |  Your
        Tools       |  Position
                    |
                Low Price
```

### Step 3: Identify Your Unique Value

**What can ONLY you claim?**

| Differentiator Type | Example |
|---------------------|---------|
| **Feature** | Only product with X capability |
| **Approach** | Unique methodology or process |
| **Focus** | Only solution for X niche |
| **Expertise** | Founded by world experts in X |
| **Experience** | Simplest/fastest/easiest |
| **Results** | Proven X% improvement |
| **Integration** | Works with X ecosystem |

**Find your "only":**
```
We are the ONLY [category]
that [unique thing]
for [specific audience].
```

### Step 4: Choose Your Category

**Strategic category choice:**

| Strategy | When to Use | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Existing category** | Clear market, easy to understand | "Project management software" |
| **Sub-category** | Differentiate in crowded market | "Async project management for remote teams" |
| **New category** | Creating new space | "Revenue intelligence platform" |
| **Versus** | Challenger positioning | "The [X] for [specific audience]" |

**Category creation:**
Only create new category if:
- Existing categories don't fit
- You can define and educate market
- The category can be bigger than you

### Step 5: Craft Your Positioning Statement

**Internal positioning statement:**
```
For [target customer]
who [situation/need]
[Product] is the [category]
that [key benefit]
unlike [alternatives]
we [unique differentiator].
```

**External positioning (one-liner):**
```
[Product] helps [audience] to [outcome] by [unique approach].
```

**Examples:**

```
Internal:
For solo SaaS founders
who want to ship faster without a team
Faion Network is the AI development framework
that provides proven methodologies and AI agents
unlike courses or just tools
we combine education with working software.

External:
Faion Network helps solo founders ship products 10x faster
with AI agents and proven methodologies.
```

### Step 6: Validate Your Positioning

**Testing methods:**

| Method | How |
|--------|-----|
| Customer interviews | "How would you describe us to a peer?" |
| Competitor comparison | "What makes us different from X?" |
| Headline testing | A/B test positioning messages |
| Sales conversations | What resonates, what doesn't? |

**Positioning is strong when:**
- Customers can repeat it
- It disqualifies wrong-fit prospects
- It resonates emotionally
- It's hard for competitors to copy

## Examples

### Example 1: Developer Tool

**Before (weak):**
```
A modern code editor for developers.
```

**After (strong):**
```
For developers who hate context switching,
Cursor is the AI-first code editor
that understands your entire codebase
unlike VS Code or Copilot alone
we make AI your pair programmer, not just autocomplete.
```

### Example 2: SaaS Analytics

**Before (weak):**
```
Analytics for your business.
```

**After (strong):**
```
For bootstrapped founders who need insights without data science,
Plausible is the privacy-friendly analytics
that shows what matters in under 60 seconds
unlike Google Analytics
we're simple, fast, and GDPR-compliant out of the box.
```

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Too broad audience | Message resonates with no one | Get more specific |
| Feature-based | Easily copied | Position on value/approach |
| Claiming too much | Unbelievable | Pick one thing to own |
| Inside-out thinking | Customers don't care | Start with their problem |
| Changing constantly | No recognition built | Commit for 12+ months |
| Ignoring alternatives | Miss competitive frame | Position against something |

---

## Positioning Health Check

**Ask yourself:**

| Question | Good Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| Can customers repeat our positioning? | In their words, correctly |
| Does it disqualify some prospects? | Yes, intentionally |
| Is it defensible for 2+ years? | Yes, not easily copied |
| Does it make sales easier? | Fewer objections |
| Does it guide product decisions? | Clear priorities |
